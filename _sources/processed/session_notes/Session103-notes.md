# The Dragon’s Keep, Briara’s Confession, and the Black-Ice Bridge
## Session Overview
This session picked up with a recap of the previous chapter’s major events: the party’s fight through trolls and cats, their conversation with Briara, the skull-lantern barricade, and the battle at the dragon’s keep. The recap emphasized that the dragon had animated the surrounding statues, and that Riven ultimately shot the dragon down, which caused the remaining statues to stop moving. After the party looted the keep, the tone shifted into a deeper social and narrative discussion about Briara, Nerissa, Liriel, and what to do next.

The middle of the session focused on the group weighing whether to rest or push onward. Briara’s sudden emotional shift revealed that Nerissa was close, and that Briara could feel “the shape of where I used to be.” The party questioned Briara about her split nature, whether she wanted to be reunited, and what that would mean for Nerissa, Briara, and the Lantern King’s curse. They then used the keep as a secure rest point, debated whether magical dimensional effects might be unstable in this pocket-realm, and chose to long rest.

After the rest and morning preparations, the party traveled north through a darker, colder path to reach Nerissa’s abode at the edge of time. They found a bridge over black, space-like water, and after discussing the danger and the distance, they crossed onto the black-ice bridge. That immediately triggered an encounter with Tarlaxion, a two-headed guardian beast with extreme mobility and a devastating frost-based kit. The fight became a chaotic bridge battle involving flight, forced movement, healing, positioning, and repeated attempts to avoid being dragged into the water. The session ended on a cliffhanger mid-combat, with the party still trying to get across the bridge and survive Tarlaxion’s attacks.
## Detailed Story Events
- Dragon’s keep and surrounding barricade: The session opened as a recap of the previous game’s events. The party discussed defeating the trolls, speaking with Briara, getting through a barricade with skull-faced lanterns, and reaching a keep surrounded by statues. The dragon at the keep had an ability to animate the statues, turning the battlefield into a layered fight where the party had to protect the “squishies” while focusing on the dragon.
  - Pavel summarized that the keep had “a very cool three-dimensionality” and that the new version’s vertical line-of-sight rules mattered during the fight.
  - Riven delivered the killing shot: “Riven caught it with an arrow at the last second and brought it down,” and once the dragon fell, “the remaining statues stopped animating.”
  - The recap concluded with the party having looted the keep afterward, with Pavel noting that there were “some new toys in the stash.”

- Keep after the dragon fight: While the party discussed whether to rest or immediately pursue Liriel, Briara abruptly grew quiet in a way that stood out against her usual teasing and flirting. Danny described the shift as Briara becoming “very quiet, noticeably so,” before speaking in a different, more serious tone: “Oh, oh, she’s close. I can feel the shape of where I used to be.”
  - Pavel reacted to the revelation by taking Briara out and asking what she meant by Nerissa being near and what she meant by feeling her “shape.”
  - Briara answered that she had not felt that presence “for ages, ages and ages.”
The scene shifted from light banter into a serious discussion of identity, reunion, and what it would mean if Briara and Nerissa were made whole or separated again.

- Top of the keep tower and surrounding twilight path: Pavel climbed to the top of the tower to scout the surrounding area and see what lay ahead. He described checking for danger, Fey presence, Nerissa, or “frankly anybody with red hair.”
- From the top, he saw the path forward darken into a “patch of darker darkness,” noticeably dimmer than the usual twilight of the realm.
  - Danny clarified that it was not ordinary darkness; it was simply a more intense dimness than the rest of the area.
  - Pavel came back down and reported that he had seen no imminent danger, and that Briara might not have meant Nerissa was just outside the castle walls—she could have meant Nerissa was somewhere ahead, on the same plane, or otherwise nearby.

- Keep with Briara present: The party continued debating Briara’s fate and what to do about Nerissa and Liriel. They weighed whether destroying Briara would be the right move, whether returning her to Nerissa might help, and how the Lantern King’s bargains and punishments worked.
  - Pavel referenced notes and journal entries to piece together contradictory information about Briara and Nerissa.
  - The group discussed the possibility that Briara could be returned to Nerissa, destroyed, or otherwise used as part of the larger curse.
Despite the uncertainty, the group did not commit to a final course of action and instead settled on resting before proceeding.

- Keep in the meta-dimension/twilight: Before resting, the party talked through their magical preparations. Lucas nearly cast a planar spell, but Astor warned that dimensional magic had already been troublesome earlier, especially when teleportation had acted strangely in the earlier troll-and-cats fight.
  - The party explicitly noted that they were still in a “meta dimension” or pocket-realm and that trying to open another dimensional space might be risky.
  - Lucas paused and decided not to cast the spell.
  - The group chose to rest for the night rather than risk further dimensional complications.
- Keep and path north: After the long rest, the party made morning preparations and set out north. Danny described them with backpacks on, “buckled and ready to go,” marching for about an hour as the path dimmed and the air grew colder.
  - The party rounded a bend and found “the house at the edge of time.”
  - The house was revealed to be reachable by a bridge, and the place itself seemed to sit “out at the edge of space and time, unanchored from the world.”
  - Briara fell silent, apparently because the presence she had sensed was now too close for her usual comments.

- Black-ice bridge over the bottomless lake: The party assessed the only path to Nerissa’s abode. Pavel inspected the water and described it as “space water,” warning the others to go first.
  - Danny explained that the bridge was about 300 feet long and covered in black ice, with no guard rails and a significant chance of slipping off into the lake.
  - The party discussed flight, potions, cloaks, and the possibility of bypassing the bridge, but ultimately prepared to cross.
  - Pavel moved forward first. The moment he stepped onto the bridge, an enormous creature burst out of the water and landed directly in front of him, initiating combat.
  - Initiative was rolled, and the creature was revealed to be Tarlaxion, the bridge’s guardian. Danny described it as a two-headed monstrosity that guards the crossing and must be dealt with by anyone who wants to pass.
  - Pavel used lore about the creature to warn the party that killing it would curse them for a year and prevent healing.
  - Tarlaxion demonstrated its abilities in a brutal opening sequence: rhyme breath, frostbite aura, bites, grabs, and coil drag. The party spread out, used flight, and tried to manage the bridge’s slippery terrain while also dealing with the water below.
  - Astor used a lucky rabbit’s foot to reroll a failed save and then shoved Tarlaxion, trying to drive it off the bridge. The black ice caused forced movement to become extremely dangerous.
  - The party repeatedly adjusted positions and used flight or jumps to avoid being pulled into the lake.
  - Lee was later grabbed and dragged off the bridge into the water, prompting panic and rescue attempts.
  - Pavel used movement and healing support to help Lee, while Lucas and others tried to keep themselves alive through healing and repositioning.
  - The monster’s attacks continued to focus heavily on the party’s front line and anyone still within its reach.
  - The session ended with the group still in the middle of the bridge fight, deciding whether to keep pressing forward or pause until the next session.

Out of combat, he was part of the party’s stash discussions and later provided a silver tongue mutagen to help with the coming negotiation.

- Briara: Briara’s importance increased significantly this session. She stopped joking and became deeply serious when she sensed Nerissa’s proximity.
She stated that she could feel “the shape of where I used to be.”
She described herself as torn between the idea of being whole again and the fear of being reunited with her “worst half.”
She confirmed that she had been torn out of herself as punishment “for love.”
Her condition, relationship to Nerissa, and possible restoration or destruction became one of the session’s primary mysteries.

## Plot Threads and Mysteries
- Briara and Nerissa’s split identity: The biggest ongoing mystery. The party still does not know whether Briara should be destroyed, restored, or returned to Nerissa, and whether that would help or doom them.
- The Lantern King’s bargain: The group discussed conflicting accounts of what the Lantern King promised, including that Briara would be returned as an instrument of Nerissa’s death and that Nerissa’s love might be restored if she completes her goals.
- Liriel’s kidnapping: Still unresolved and still pressing the party to move quickly.
- The nature of Nerissa’s abode: The house at the edge of time appears to be a guarded, surreal place only reachable by the black-ice bridge.
- Tarlaxion’s domain: The party inferred that the creature likely controls the bridge and the lake and may not pursue beyond that boundary.
- The curse from killing Tarlaxion: Pavel warned that killing it would curse the party for a year and prevent healing.
- Dimensional instability: The party remained cautious after earlier teleportation issues and avoided casting planar magic again.
