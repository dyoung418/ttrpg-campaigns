6. ### Wes [Riven] _—_ 2/1/25, 6:16 AM
    
    The Feywind Shop still sat nestled in the heart of Nightmarch, shaded by the growing maples and oaks, cooled by the river breeze. Though the city bustled around it, the shop remained wild—untamed in a way that Riven had always liked. Starberries thrived along the walls, their sweet scent mingling with sawdust and oiled wood. Brumble dozed in the shade, his steady snoring a familiar presence. Riven hadn’t worked the shop in years, though he still checked in when he could. He had turned it over to skilled hands—Ekundayo, with his sharp eyes and famous tripline arrows, and now Thaelirian, a master bowyer whose work was reknowned. Mist still clung to the ground as Riven pushed open the wooden door. It groaned on its hinges, same as always. Inside, Thaelirian was already at work, his carving knife gliding across raw maple, stripping away the excess. He didn’t look up right away—just finished a careful cut before finally acknowledging Riven with a glance. Riven stepped inside, his gaze flicking over the unfinished bow, its form just beginning to take shape. "Thaelirian. It’s good to see you. You waste no time, I see." "I never do." Thaelirian’s voice was dry, but there was the faintest trace of a smile. "Three bows last week. Four, if I finish this one today. Cassandra requested them for her troops." Riven's smile curled with amusement. "Cassandra is stubborn as iron. Took me months to convince her that a halberd isn’t always the answer. But seeing your work seems to have done the trick." Thaelirian simply nodded and returned to his carving.
    
7. Riven ran a hand along the curve of a finished longbow, tracing the delicate blend of strength and grace. There was an artistry to it, something alive in the wood that each movement from the bowyer released. His fingers hesitated, then stilled. "Thaelirian—I need your help." The ranger barely paused. "You were at the feast. You know what we faced." Riven’s voice was quiet, but heavy with the weight of it. "And I don’t think it’s over. If anything, I think it’s just the beginning." Thaelirian finally set his carving knife down. "You expect something worse than a lich?" Riven exhaled through his nose, shaking his head. "I expect everything. Enemies we haven’t seen yet. Threats we can’t name. This kingdom… It stands on the edge of something, and I don’t know if we’re ready." A pause. Then: "And you’ve come to me because…?"
    
8. Riven unslung his longbow—Feywind—and laid it on the worktable. The wood was worn beneath his grip, the runes etched into its surface flickering faintly with magic. "Because I need this to be more. I need it to strike harder and truer. I need to rain down wrath upon our enemies." Thaelirian picked up the bow, testing its weight and the draw and running his fingers along its surface as if listening to something only he could hear. "A flame rune… some power." He muttered it more to himself than to Riven. "Yes. You will need more. I can weave an Astral rune into it and enhance its power, sharpen its vengeance." "Good." "It won’t be quick. A month, at least and it won't be cheap." Riven nodded, "Good work never is." The older elf lifted his gaze, eyes flickering with something unreadable. "You ought to replace those too." Riven frowned. "Replace what?" Thaelirian tilted his chin at the battered goggles hanging from Riven’s belt. "Those have seen better days." "Know someone who can get me better ones?"
    
9. "A friend from Kyonin is coming to stay with me for a few days. I'll send word—he can bring some. It'll cost you, of course." Riven barely heard him. His mind was already racing. "A friend from Kyonin?" His voice had changed, edged with something sharper, more urgent. "Can I meet him when he comes?" Thaelirian arched a brow. "Of course. He’s—" "We found elven ruins in the forest." Riven cut in, words spilling out. "Old, untouched places. The kind that shouldn’t be forgotten. If your friend has connections back home—if I could persuade him to send a delegation, we could—" Thaelirian watched him with quiet patience as Riven's thoughts unfurled. But when it became clear the ranger wasn’t going to stop, he finally spoke. "Riven." And again more forcefully. "Riven." Riven stopped mid-thought, breathing hard. Thaelirian’s voice was quiet. "Is this truly about the ruins?" Silence. Riven looked down, his fingers tightening on the edge of the worktable. He opened his mouth. Closed it. Exhaled. "_Maybe_ I can convince Lyriel to come," he said. Thaelirian simply nodded. And without another word, he smiled and picked up his carving knife and returned to his work.
