@everyone  The inner courtyard of the Old Fortress at Nightmarch is busy in the way of things that precede terrible events — purposeful, quiet, no  wasted motion. Provisions are being checked and loaded. Two rangers near the eastern wall are testing bowstrings without speaking. A  group by the gate is leaning over a map, their voices too low to carry. Three days ago, Naryssa's forces hit a settlement to the south.  What the scouts found there, people repeat only once and then stop.

 Riven is inside with the council. Lucas is at his prayers somewhere. The morning sky is the pale, uncommitted gray of early light that  hasn't decided what it will become.

 In the far corner of the courtyard, where the old wall meets the stable block, a large black panther lies curled with his tail across his nose. Pavel is a few feet away, checking the lashings on his pack — each one, in order, with the focused economy of a man who has  done this ten thousand times and does it again anyway because that is the kind of man he is.

 Edina Aldori has been watching from the corridor archway for longer than she planned. She had told herself she would approach him when she was ready. She is beginning to understand that ready is not somewhere she is going to arrive on her own.

She steps out of the archway. The boots she is wearing today are new. 
She crosses the courtyard at a walking pace, on a direct line. Not cautious, not stealthy. She knows better than to approach this  particular man the way one approaches a skittish animal — he would read that immediately, and he would resent it, and he would be right  to. She has thought carefully about how to do this. Directness is not always kindness, but with Pavel, she suspects, it comes closer  than most alternatives.

She stops a few feet away. Not too far — that would be a retreat. Not so close it feels like a maneuver. The panther opens one blue  eye, considers her, and closes it again.

"Pavel." Just his name. No other preamble. Enough to make clear that she came specifically to find him, and that she found what she was  looking for.

She holds his gaze steadily. Her eyes are gray, and they give very little back.

 "I know you've been hoping to get out of this courtyard before anyone talked to you." A dry note in it, not unkind — just honest. The  acknowledgment of something real. "I won't keep you long."

 She doesn't look at his pack, or his weapons, or the gate. She looks at him.

 "I owe you an apology. For the feast." One beat. "I should have done more to put you at ease.  Those were old boots, actually. I'd been meaning to replace them for months."
