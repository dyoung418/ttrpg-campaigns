## The Mechanic: Switched Backstories

Each player goes through the standard Daggerheart PC creation flow as normal — class, heritage, backstory questions, connections, everything. They do this **in isolation** (no cross-table sharing).

Once all backstories are collected, **secretly redistribute them** before Session 1. Player A's PC wakes up in Session 1 living Player B's backstory, Player B's PC has Player C's, and so on. The players don't know the switch happened.

**Why this works:**
- Players genuinely don't know who their PC is — because even the backstory they created belongs to someone else's character
- As PCs uncover memories, players will slowly recognize details from *their own* creation — and realize another PC is living the life they designed
- The cross-recognition creates emergent dramatic irony without the GM needing to engineer it

**Running the session:**
- Run Daggerheart character creation as written — class selection, heritage, the 3 background questions, connections between characters
- Keep the backstory question answers recorded privately (not shared at table)
- Collect written/noted answers before the session ends
- Do the swap before Session 1 prep — assign each backstory to a different PC, making sure no player gets their own
- The connections players created between characters can be partially preserved or also shuffled — your call

**Things to decide before Session 1:**
- [ ] How much of the connection web (bonds between PCs) carries over vs. gets scrambled?
- [ ] Will all backstory details surface through play, or do some remain permanently lost?
- [ ] Is there a mechanic for PCs to "reclaim" their true identity, or is the switched self who the PCs have always been, but the player's didn't know it?
- [ ] What if some of the backstory created by a player deals specifically about why their PC became a wizard or other class?  The PC that actually get's the backstory might not be that class.  Some modification by the GM might be needed.
