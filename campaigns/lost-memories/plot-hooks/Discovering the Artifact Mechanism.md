# Discovering the Artifact Mechanism

> **Status**: Open
> **Priority**: High
> **Note**: This is a GM planning document — three possible discovery seeds for how the PCs learn that their memories are stored in [[Magister Aldric]]'s artifacts. Choose one, combine them, or let the campaign find its own path.

## Summary

The PCs' memories were dispersed into objects dear to [[Magister Aldric]] when [[The Power Transference Ritual|the ritual]] failed. Neither the PCs nor Aldric knows this yet. At some point in Act 1 or early Act 2, this mechanism must become legible — the PCs need to understand not just that their memories are gone, but that they can be recovered, and where. These seeds are possible routes to that revelation.

## Discovery Seeds

### Seed A — The Ritual Notes (In the Tower)

Aldric's working notes on [[The Power Transference Ritual]] are somewhere in [[The Wizard's Prison]], written for himself by a brilliant man. The theory is stated plainly: power is embedded in lived memory, and memory requires an anchor to transfer. If the transference fails, the memories seek the nearest available anchor — objects with strong emotional resonance present at the time of the ritual.

His notes don't say *my objects*. But the PCs are smart enough to ask: whose strong emotional resonance was in that ritual chamber? The answer is standing in the room with them.

**GM notes:**
- The notes don't need to spell it out — leave the final logical step for the players
- Works best if the PCs have time to explore the ritual chamber before or during their escape
- The notes could also inadvertently reveal that the ritual has been attempted before, opening the door to the previous subjects as a thread

### Seed B — The Rival Magister Is Already Holding One (Post-Escape)

The suspicious colleague in the [[Order of the Silver Chalice]] has been quietly investigating [[Magister Aldric]] for years. In that work, one of Aldric's objects passed through their hands — routed through a third party, logged in a transfer, given as a gift — and they experienced a vivid flash of a life that wasn't theirs. They didn't know what to make of it and filed it away.

When the PCs reach the Order and explain what was done to them, the rival Magister recognizes the description. *That flash I had — that was one of yours.* They still have the object. The PCs' first recovered memory arrives from a stranger's pocket.

**GM notes:**
- Requires the rival Magister NPC to be developed before the PCs reach the Order — decide their trustworthiness and agenda in advance
- This seed also gives the rival Magister a reason to believe the PCs immediately, which can help establish them as an ally
- The object they're holding is a hook in itself: which PC's memory is it? What does it show?

### Seed C — The Tower Is Already Haunted (In the Tower)

Aldric keeps some of his dearest objects close — he works in the tower, and the objects are dear to him. Weird things have been happening that he has attributed to ritual instability: a guard who woke from a dream about a life that wasn't his, an attendant who cried in a room for no reason she could name, a persistent feeling in certain hallways that something is watching without eyes.

The PCs can notice the pattern before they understand it. The tower isn't haunted by ghosts. It's haunted by scattered selves. The question changes from *can we recover our memories?* to *why does this place feel so strangely lived-in?*

**GM notes:**
- Works as ambient texture from Session 1 onward — no single revelation, just accumulating wrongness
- Pairs naturally with Seed A: the PCs feel the haunting first, then read the notes and understand why
- The guard or attendant who experienced flashes is a potential in-tower ally — someone who has been disturbed by what they've witnessed and might be persuaded to help

## What the Players Know

They have no memory. They are prisoners. Something was done to them here — the ritual chamber is evidence of that. Whether and how they discover the artifact mechanism depends on which seeds the GM uses.

## What's Actually Happening

> [!secret] GM Only
> The memories dispersed into objects weighted with Aldric's own sentimentality — things from his personal history that were present at the ritual or already existed in the world when it was performed. The objects are passive vessels. Contact is enough to trigger fragments. The PCs could begin recovering themselves the moment they touch the right thing — they just don't know it yet.

## A Connection You Noticed

The ritual was designed to extract power from lived experience — to steal the accumulated weight of a life fully practiced, to skip the decades of work. The whole campaign is about the PCs doing the opposite: earning themselves back from scratch, with no context, one fragment at a time. Aldric wanted the shortcut. The campaign is the proof that it doesn't exist. That's not backstory — that's the theme. Every session the PCs spend figuring out who they are is an argument against everything Aldric believes.

## A Question Worth Sitting With

The memories are stored in things dear to Aldric — objects weighted with his sentimentality, his history, his loss. Recovering a piece of yourself means obtaining something he values. So: is getting themselves back something he has to lose? And what does it mean to a man who has decided people are resources — when the PCs force him to choose between his treasures and his ambition? Does he give anything up willingly? Does he even know what he's carrying?

## Possible Outcomes

- **If the PCs discover the mechanism early (in the tower)**: They escape knowing what they're looking for — artifacts. The hunt becomes a through-line for the whole campaign.
- **If discovery is delayed (post-escape)**: The early campaign is disorientation and survival; the revelation that recovery is *possible* becomes a midpoint turning point.
- **If the PCs learn the mechanism before Aldric does**: A brief window of asymmetric knowledge — they know something about his ritual that he doesn't. Use it.
