# The Switched Backstories

> **Status**: Open
> **Priority**: High
> **Note**: This is a GM technique, not an in-world event. See below.

## What This Is (GM Only)

This is a Session 0 meta-game technique, not something that happened in the world. In-world, the PCs simply have amnesia caused by [[The Power Transference Ritual]]. Their true backstories — who they actually are — exist and are recoverable.

The technique: each player ghost-writes the *real* backstory for a *different* player's PC, without knowing that's what they're doing. They believe they're creating their own character's history. The GM collects these backstories in isolation and assigns them across the group before Session 1.

**The result**: As the PCs recover memories during play, the players will slowly recognize details — names, places, relationships — that *they* invented. But those details belong to someone else's character. Player A will realize that the life PC B is piecing together is the one they wrote. The dramatic irony is entirely at the player level.

## What the Players Know (In-World)

Their PC has lost all memory. They are piecing together fragments — images, names, feelings — but nothing is confirmed. The other PCs seem to be in the same situation.

## The In-World Truth

There is no identity swap. The PCs are exactly who they are. [[Magister Aldric]]'s failed [[The Power Transference Ritual|Power Transference Ritual]] erased their memories — their true selves are simply inaccessible, stored in artifacts scattered by the ritual. The "switched" quality of the backstories is a player-level experience only.

## Session 0 Execution Notes

- Players create backstories **in isolation** — no sharing at the table
- Players submit backstory answers privately to the GM
- Players suggest possible bonds with other PCs (the GM will assign these after the swap)
- The GM **pre-screens backstories** before assigning them — lightly editing any details that reference a specific class ability or background that doesn't match the receiving PC's class
- The GM assigns bonds using the players' suggestions, mixing and matching as needed
- Formal bond creation is skipped at Session 0; the GM architects the connections and they emerge through play

## Hidden Community Mechanic (Session 1+)

Because community assignments are switched along with backstories, the players do not know their PC's true community at the start of play.

**At the moment the PCs wake with no memories (Session 1):** the GM physically takes the community cards from each player. The players' instinct about which community their character belongs to is wrong — it belongs to someone else, just like the backstory.

**What the PC still has access to:** domain cards and class abilities are fully intact. The PC instinctually knows how to use all of their abilities — including their community ability. The *player*, however, doesn't know what that ability is.

**The reveal mechanic:** the GM watches for situations in play where a community ability would naturally trigger. When one arises, the GM narrates to that player: *"Your character instinctually does [ability]"* — and hands them their community card in that moment. The reveal is organic, not announced.

**GM implication:** build early scenes with moments that could plausibly activate a range of community abilities. Don't force it — but create the soil for it. Each player should get their community card through lived experience, not a handout.

**Important:** the PC *in-world* always had this ability. The reveal is entirely a player-level experience. The amnesia is the cover story; the community card is just arriving late to the table.

## The Dramatic Payoff

As memories surface during the campaign, moments of recognition will hit players organically:
- *"Wait — I wrote about a sister named Vael. That's... my character didn't write that."*
- *"That location they just described — I invented that place."*

No GM engineering required. The irony arrives on its own.
