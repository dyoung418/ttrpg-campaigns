# The Power Transference Ritual

> **Category**: Magic — experimental ritual
> **Source**: Invented by [[Magister Aldric]]
> **Status**: Unproven; still being refined

## What It Is

A new ritual developed entirely by [[Magister Aldric]] with no precedent in established magical tradition. The core theory: power and ability do not merely reside in a person's mind or body — they are *accrued* through lived experience, embedded in memory, identity, and the accumulated weight of a life fully practiced. The ritual is designed to extract this accrued power and transfer it to the caster.

## What It Was Supposed to Do

Subject a skilled, accomplished individual to the ritual and drain the power embedded in their lived mastery — transferring those abilities, talents, and accumulated skill to [[Magister Aldric]].

## What Actually Happened

The ritual failed. [[Magister Aldric]] gained none of the PCs' abilities or powers. The power transference did not occur.

**The side effect**: The ritual *did* do something — it severed the PCs' connection to their memories. Rather than transferring their power to [[Magister Aldric]], the ritual dispersed the PCs' memories into objects *dear to Aldric* — things with sentimental or symbolic weight to him. Some of these artifacts were in the tower when the ritual occurred; others were scattered to wherever they already existed in the world.

Neither [[Magister Aldric]] nor the PCs know this yet. The discovery mechanism — how both sides come to understand that memories are stored in artifacts — is TBD. A possible seed: previous subjects in the tower have accidentally received memory flashes from touching objects, which the PCs may witness or experience themselves.

## Aldric's Current Understanding

- He knows the power transfer failed entirely
- He has since discovered the memory loss in his subjects and is simultaneously fascinated and furious
- He does not yet understand that the memories dispersed into his personal artifacts
- He believes the ritual's theoretical foundation is sound but there is a flaw in the execution — he is actively working to fix it
- He will need powerful subjects to re-attempt the ritual, but not necessarily the same ones; the PCs remain desirable targets
