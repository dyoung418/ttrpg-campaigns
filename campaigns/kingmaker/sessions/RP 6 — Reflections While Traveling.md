# RP 6 — Reflections While Traveling

> **Source**: RP6 (long traveling conversation; Astor plays [[Benedin]])
> **Status**: `rp-scene`
> **Players present**: [[Riven]], [[Pavel]], [[Astor]] (as [[Benedin]])
> **Setting**: Crossing the [[Nomen Heights]] toward the [[Tors of Levenies]], escorted by two Nomen centaur warriors.

## Summary

Marching toward Vordakai's domain under centaur escort, [[Riven]] voices his unease about [[Varnhold]]'s fate and a deeper emptiness he can't shake despite all the party's great deeds. [[Pavel]], [[Benedin]], and Riven trade philosophies on judgment, duty, and what they're really fighting for.

## Key Beats

**Riven on Varnhold.** He condemns Varnhold's leaders for delving into ancient evils and for the flayed Nomen centaur skins — *"people like that don't deserve to rule"* — but admits to a hollowness inside: he came to do great deeds and has (Stag Lord, Cult of the Bloom, Hargulka), yet it never filled him.

**Benedin pushes back.** [[Benedin]] warns against judging the dead before knowing their story, then shares his own: his dwarven clan held these mountains before the name Aldori was known; he trained as clan **lorekeeper** until trolls with unexpected magic (veils, glamours, stone-shaping) overran them.

**Pavel's wisdom.** Pavel reflects that long-lived elves build up imagined futures and suffer when reality disappoints — *"my mother felt that way."* He, by contrast, expected to die young, so everything is a gift. He concludes you can't judge yourself fairly; *"the best people to do it are your friends."*

**Riven's turn toward hope.** Riven confesses guilt *"from long ago, from choices I made that can't be undone"* — but then brightens: *"I found hope in our friendship. You matter more to me than the adventures... I think [[Amara]] was right."* He asks Pavel about his mother and Astor about his people.

**The centaurs overhear.** The two stern escorts' faces soften from disapproval to appreciation — centaurs hear better than Riven knew.
