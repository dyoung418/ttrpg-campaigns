# Session 104 — The First Mercy

> **Date**:
> **Status**: Planned
> **Previous Session**: [[Session 103 - The Shape of Where I Used to Be]]

> [!note] Where this sits in the endgame
> This is the **run-up**, not the finale. With combat running long, the whole
> [[House at the Edge of Time]] is a **4–5 session arc**. Session 104's only jobs are:
> **(1)** finish the Rimebridge and get off it, and **(2)** deliver the *first forgiveness
> lesson* at the gate — planting the seed for the campaign's central choice about [[Nyrissa]].
> Don't rush toward the Queen; there's a whole House between here and her.

---

## The Endgame Spine *(read this first)*

The two climaxes — **rescuing [[Lyriel]]** and **the choice about [[Nyrissa]]** — are the
same story. Nyrissa is a woman whose capacity for love was *cut out of her* and forged into
[[Briar|Briara]] (now speaking at [[Pavel]]'s hip). [[Riven]] is a man who *threw love away*
and has spent the campaign learning that redemption is internal, not earned by deeds. Lyriel —
healer, innocent — is the living proof of what Riven reclaimed.

**How forgiveness surfaces naturally** (the worry that shaped this prep): the House is run as a
**graduated "kill-or-forgive" tutorial**, so mercy is a discovered, practiced option long before
the Queen — never a surprise rules-lookup at the final boss.

1. **The Gatehouse (this session)** — the ashamed dead. First contact with a *Shame* creature
   and the **Forgive Foe** action. The party learns: *some "monsters" here want release, not death.*
2. **[[The Wriggling Man]] (mid-crawl, holds Lyriel)** — the counter-example. No Shame; a deceiver
   and the [[The Lantern King|Lantern King]]'s spy. Killing him is unambiguously *right*. Teaches
   the party to ask which kind of enemy they're facing.
3. **Lyriel's rescue (mid-crawl)** — the thesis spoken aloud. The healer names what she's sensed
   inside this grief-soaked place: a queen who is *hollow, not hateful.* Her line to Riven —
   *"Some hurts can't be cut out. You can only forgive them"* — is the argument for mercy, from the
   person who is the proof of his own redemption. **Lyriel stays separate from Nyrissa** — her rescue
   is its own co-equal climax, and it happens *before* the confrontation so it can inform the choice.
4. **The capstone** — trapped captives like the K19 Bythos Aeons are *freed only if Nyrissa is
   killed **or** forgiven.* Either ending frees the House's prisoners — quietly reframing the final
   question as *who Nyrissa is*, not whether the party "wins."

## Step 4 — Secrets and Clues

> [!secret] GM Only — keep abstract from discovery method

1. The ashamed dead are the elves of **Nyrissa's first, failed kingdom** — the one she couldn't save. They guard the Queen they have every reason to hate.
2. **Forgiving a foe here can *free* it.** The House is full of prisoners, not just monsters — mercy is a weapon that works on the things Nyrissa has bound.
3. The ghosts suffer *Shame* before elves and Calistrians — they physically **cannot bring themselves to truly strike [[Riven]] or [[Lee]]**.
4. [[Nyrissa]] watches the entire House from a hidden inner sanctum and can **kill her own servants the instant they help the party** — her allies dread her more than they dread the PCs.
5. **[[Lyriel]] is alive**, held deeper in the House — leverage, not a trophy. Nyrissa mined Riven's dreams and took her the moment the relationship was healing.
6. The House **resets** when the party leaves [[Thousandbreaths]] — doors reform, traps rebuild, the living heal — **but the dead stay dead and treasure taken stays taken.** Every guardian freed or killed is permanent.
7. **Many of the House's "monsters" are Nyrissa's grief, not her loyalty** — her dead, her blinded handmaids, champions who failed — and several can be *freed by forgiving them* rather than killed.
8. [[Tarlaxian]] was never Nyrissa's servant — a squatter she tolerated because he drowned her guests. **He never had to be killed at all;** the party read that correctly.
9. **[[Briar\|Briara]] goes silent this close to Nyrissa because she can feel herself being pulled home** — and she is *afraid* of being swallowed back into what abandoned her.
10. The one who took Lyriel **wears many faces** — a worm that walks, disguised. *(Seed for [[The Wriggling Man]] later.)*

### The gate as a fight *and* a lesson
The Ghostly Guards are a real Creature-15 threat if the party just brawls (8 of them, incorporeal,
draining). But the **designed win is Forgive Foe**, and the session's whole point is that the party
*discovers this is possible.* Ways to surface it without hand-holding:
- **Recall Knowledge** (Religion/Society) reveals the *Shame* entry: "they can't bear to strike their
  own kind — and they don't want to be here."
- **Play the flinch.** Narrate the guards raising spears at Riven and Lee and *failing to close* —
  visibly straining against something. A perceptive PC reads it.
- **[[Lucas]] is the engine.** His Diplomacy + social kit make him the natural first mover; a single
  successful Forgive Foe (even non-crit −2) shows the mechanic has teeth, and a crit *frees* a ghost
  on-screen — the proof of concept for the entire endgame.
- **Reward the mercy:** a freed guard, before dissolving, can leave a **whisper** — a warning about
  the House watching, or a half-word about *"the one with many faces who keeps the elf-woman."*
  (See Step 8 — this is the session's real "reward.")

*Tarlaxian boss note (carried from 103): a lone Creature 20 loses the action-economy war, so his
second front is himself and the lake. **Curse of Death is drama, not a gotcha** — give the party a
beat to realize what landing the kill costs, and remember passing is a full win.*

## GM Notes

> [!warning] GM Only
> - **This session teaches a verb: *forgive*.** Everything else (the bridge, the portcullis) is
>   staging for the moment a player realizes they can free a "monster" instead of killing it. Protect
>   that beat. If they brute-force the whole gate, that's a valid choice — but make sure they *saw*
>   the option and turned it down, so the Nyrissa choice later isn't a surprise.
> - **Don't rush past the bridge.** Combat is slow; finishing Tarlaxian may be a big chunk of the
>   night on its own. The gate can slide to the top of 105 if needed — the strong start there writes
>   itself (the spears rising).
> - **Curse of Death is a decision, not a trap.** Telegraph it. Passing without the kill is the
>   party reading the situation correctly, not "missing" a boss kill.
> - **Play Briara's silence.** Her absence of chatter is a held breath; the players who know her will
>   feel it. Let her stir with a single quiet line once they're through the gate — the House is her
>   old home, and she half-remembers it.
> - **Riven & Lee at the gate.** Lean on the *Shame* fiction: the dead of a kingdom that died raising
>   spears they can't bring themselves to throw at their own kind. This is the emotional rhyme for the
>   whole endgame — victims of Nyrissa's failure, freed by mercy.
> - **Capture anything new [[Briar\|Briara]] says** in her note, and anything the freed dead reveal.
> - **Where next:** past the gate is the Great Courtyard (K2) and the House proper. [[Lyriel]] and
>   [[The Wriggling Man]] are the mid-crawl target (throne room K17) — *not* this session.
