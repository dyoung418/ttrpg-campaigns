# RP 14.2 — Lucas and the Bards

> **Source**: RP14.2 (companion scene to the Great Feast; Danny voices [[Linzi]])
> **Status**: `rp-scene`
> **Players present**: [[Lucas]]
> **Setting**: The Great Feast at [[Nightmarch]].

## Summary

After [[Lucas]] enthralls the feast's children with story-cantrips, the legendary bard **[[Tol-Kin]]** quietly critiques his pacing, then takes the floor himself and weaves a magical tale that leaves the hall breathless. Lucas asks to become his pupil — and Tol-Kin agrees to take him (and [[Linzi]]) on the road for a month.

## Key Beats

**The critique.** An old man advises Lucas not to rush the exciting part — *"it just leaves people wanting more."* [[Linzi]] reveals he's **[[Tol-Kin]]**, one of the greatest living bards.

**The performance.** Tol-Kin tells *The Halfling, The Dragon, and the Baker's Dozen Dwarves* with otherworldly music and illusion; the crowd sits in a full minute of silence before erupting.

**The invitation.** Tol-Kin came only at the request of a trainee of his old student **[[Beneford]]** (now Court Bard in the East, [[Linzi]]'s teacher). He won't stay — *"never trust a Bard who hasn't any scars or who's happy settling down"* — but agrees to take Lucas as a pupil for a month, and invites Linzi along. He hints he may return for the tournament's **Battle of the Bards**, perhaps to tell *Frohdeaux the Nine-Fingered and the Ring of Doom*.

## Characters Present / Mentioned

- [[Lucas]] — takes up the bard's road; *(mechanically, retrains into the Bard Dedication)*
- [[Tol-Kin]] — legendary wandering bard; agrees to mentor Lucas
- [[Linzi]] — recognizes Tol-Kin; invited along; her teacher was Beneford
- [[Beneford]] — Tol-Kin's former student; the reason he came

## Locations

## Notable Details / Flags

- Establishes [[Lucas]]'s month-long apprenticeship under [[Tol-Kin]] (with [[Linzi]]) right after the feast — and the bard connection through [[Beneford]].
- Seeds the **Battle of the Bards** event of the [[Grand Tournament of Freehaven]].
