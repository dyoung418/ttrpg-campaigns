## Step 4 — Secrets and Clues

> [!secret] GM Only — keep abstract from discovery method

1. [[Lyriel]] is alive inside the [[House at the Edge of Time]] — taken as leverage, not a trophy. [[Nyrissa]] wants the party to come.
2. Nyrissa has been mining Riven's dreams — she knew Lyriel was coming before Lyriel did. The abduction was *timed*.
3. The abduction was deliberately staged to draw the party into the House now — before they grow stronger, and because Nyrissa suspects they carry [[Briar]].
4. The Black Smilodons are corrupted First World echoes of [[Cat]] — what Cat is, and where she truly comes from, is older and stranger than Pavel knows. *(Ambient unease — pays off later, do not resolve.)*
5. The Baleful Lantern's heads still dream — they belonged to earlier champions who came for Nyrissa and failed; they can murmur warnings or truths if anyone listens.
6. [[Ilthuliak]] has never lost and intends to die undefeated — ancient, proud, no retreat, no negotiation; her hoard holds something fey-made and protective.
7. The fey-made amulet in the dragon's hoard wards its wearer against the House's teleport trap — the party won't know its specific power until it matters.
8. [[Thousandbreaths]] is a wound, not a fortress — the demiplane is Nyrissa's stolen-soul grief made into geography; the glades are stages of the same sorrow.
9. The path itself is the only safe ground — straying into the thorns isn't just damage; the demiplane subtly rearranges to separate and disorient those who stray.
10. Something wore a mortal face to take her — whoever led the abduction was disguised, and was no common fey. *(Keep faint — seeds [[The Wriggling Man]] recognition for next session's throne room.)*

## GM Notes

> [!warning] GM Only
> **The troll's return — closing the gap (Option A: Burrowing emergence):**
> The troll does not come back out the door. While "regrouping" inside, it drops into the root-riddled corrupted earth and tunnels. It **erupts directly beneath the back line** ([[Riven]] / [[Milo]] / [[Lucas]]) on its return turn — no open-ground crossing, so the party's ranged firepower doesn't shred it on approach.
> - Give it **Burrow Speed 30** for the approach; it surfaces adjacent to a chosen ranged PC.
> - **Emergence Strike**: the round it breaks surface, a hit grants a free Trip or Grab, and the eruption knocks prone anyone in adjacent squares (DC 36 Reflex or fall).
> - **Telegraph it a round early** — trembling ground, dust sifting from the head-lanterns — so it feels fair, not feel-bad.
>
> **The Smilodons matter:** They are corrupted echoes of [[Cat]]. Play Cat's reaction to fighting herself as a free roleplaying signal for [[Pavel]] — don't pre-script it, just be ready.
>
> **Lantern-heads as a feature:** A PC can stop and listen (mid-fight risk, or safely in the aftermath) to draw a warning or clue from the dead who came before — vector for Secrets 5, 6, and a faint touch of 10.
>
> **Pacing:** Expect the Baleful Lantern to take most of the night. Ilthuliak, the bridge, and Tarlaxian may all slide to next session — that's fine. If the Lantern is the whole session, the bridge-glimpse becomes the cliffhanger instead of the dragon.
>
> **Lyriel's absence does the work:** She is not seen this session. Let every round of the fight carry the weight of her waiting. [[Riven]] knows what's at stake; he doesn't have to say it.
>
> **The Wriggling Man:** Keep him entirely off-screen. The recognition beat belongs to next session's throne room (K17). Secret 10 should stay faint.
