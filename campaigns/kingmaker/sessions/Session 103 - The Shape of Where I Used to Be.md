## Step 4 — Secrets and Clues

> [!secret] GM Only — keep abstract from discovery method

1. **[[Briar\|Briara]] is the love the Eldest tore out of [[Nyrissa]] and forged into a blade** — the closer she comes to the House, the more she remembers being *part* of the Heartless Queen.
2. **Briara fears the reunion as much as she longs for it** — she doesn't know whether returning to Nyrissa would heal the Queen or simply be swallowed back into the thing that abandoned her.
3. **[[Nyrissa]] watches the entire House from a hidden inner sanctum and can kill her own servants the instant they help the party** — her allies dread her more than they dread the PCs.
4. **The House resets when the party leaves [[Thousandbreaths]]** — doors reform, traps rebuild, the living heal — *but the dead stay dead*, and treasure taken stays taken. Every guardian killed is gone for good.
5. **The House punishes any magical teleport inside it, and the party has no ward** — the protection they needed was the fey amulet they left in the dragon's hoard.
6. **Many of the House's "monsters" are Nyrissa's grief, not her loyalty** — her first kingdom's dead, her blinded handmaids, champions who failed — and several can be *freed by forgiving them* rather than killed.

### Boss design note — why Tarlaxian needed help
A lone Creature 20 loses the action-economy war against six honed PCs (they found Ilthuliak + 9 stone guardians "tough but not scary-tough"). Tarlaxian is a **pure wall** — no adds — so his second front is *himself and the lake*: **two turns/round** (the core fix), **Submerge** to deny [[Riven]]'s line of sight, **Coil Drag** to peel a PC into the bottomless lake (the action-economy reverser — *scary-but-recoverable*, never auto-lethal), a **Frostbite Aura** + the **iced bridge** to punish crowding and cap [[Lee]]'s mobility, and a fully **physical/non-verbal** kit so [[Pavel]]'s mobile *silence* whiffs. [[Lucas]]'s fascination genuinely lands (Will is his weak save) but the escape valve is to **submerge and break line of sight** — it buys a round, not the fight.

> [!warning] Lethality
> *Coil Drag is scary-but-recoverable by design* — a drowning/cold crisis that forces a 2-PC rescue, never an instant kill. But **Curse of Death (DC 46 Will — can't ever heal again)** falls on whoever lands the killing blow; let someone realize that before they commit to the kill shot. Regeneration 15 means real damage or **cold iron** ([[Briar\|Briara]] is cold iron) keeps him down.

## GM Notes

> [!warning] GM Only
> **Briara is the spine of the session.** Play her as Samantha-from-*Her* with a grudge: genuine intimacy, real feelings, a theatrical needy streak that turns on a dime. She opens the night (the cold open is hers), narrates the crossing, and seeds Secrets 1–2 — how much she *volunteers* vs. has to be coaxed is your dial. **Capture anything new she reveals in [[Briar]].**
> **The rest fork:** Let the party actually decide. Riven pushes to go now; resting secretly gates the level-up (don't tell them). Either way, the bridge is next.
> **Tarlaxian is "pure wall" by GM fiat** — he *has* Fly 100 and *could* kite like Ilthuliak, but he doesn't leave the bridge/lake. Enforce that in the fiction so the fight stays a crossing, not a chase.
> **Coil Drag is a crisis, not a kill** — telegraph the grab, let the rescue land. Keep the second turn as your pacing dial: drowning party → skip/soften it; cruising → use everything.
> **The death curse is drama, not a gotcha** — give the party a beat to realize what landing the kill costs.
> **Lyriel's absence does the work** — she's not seen; every round of the bridge is a round she waits.
> **End on the portcullis.** Tarlaxian falls, they cross, the gate grinds up, eight elves in ghostlight raise their spears. Cut to black.
