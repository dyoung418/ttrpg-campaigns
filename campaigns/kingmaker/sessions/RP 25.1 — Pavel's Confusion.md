# RP 25.1 — Pavel's Confusion

> **Source**: RP25.1 (companion scene to RP25, the [[Pavel and Edina]] courtyard confession — Pavel processing [[Edina Aldori|Edina]]'s words with [[Astor]])
> **Status**: `rp-scene`
> **Players present**: [[Pavel]], [[Astor]] (also mentioned: [[Riven]], [[Milo]], [[Lee]], [[Lucas]])
> **Setting**: The hills above [[Oaktop]], a moonless night, on the eve of an ambush against [[Nyrissa]]'s forces looting the silver mine.

## Summary

On a hilltop overlooking the ruined village of [[Oaktop]] — burned to ash by [[Nyrissa]]'s forces, its silver mine now worked by redcaps, trolls, and satyrs under lantern-light — [[Pavel]] sits and broods about women instead of the coming battle. He has already silently killed the hilltop guards, leaving the valley blind. As [[Astor]] clanks up to coordinate the ambush ([[Riven]]'s forces will feint from the west to draw the enemy out), Pavel can't stop replaying his morning conversation with [[Edina Aldori|Edina]] (the RP25 courtyard confession — see [[Pavel and Edina]]).

So he asks Astor — a married man — what it means when a woman says she wants to be *friends*. Astor brews travel-sized tea while the battle kicks off around them and tries to answer. The conversation carries all the way into a pitched battle that goes badly: [[Nyrissa]]'s garrison turns out to be a disciplined **counter-ambush** commanded by a First World **[[The Oaktop Ankou|Ankou]]**, Pavel is dropped by lightning and revived by [[Great Aunt Sumi]], and the scene ends with him — having privately decided he's too afraid to face what [[Edina Aldori|Edina]] means — charging the thirty-foot fey general head-on.

## Key Beats

**The question.** *"Astor, you understand women, right?"* Pavel wants to know what it means when a woman says she wants to be friends. Astor, distracted and pouring tea badly, admits *"I told my now-wife I wanted to be friends, because I was scared it might be too much to ask for more."* Then he turns it back on Pavel: what would it mean if *Pavel* said it?

**Pavel's blind spot.** Pavel thinks hard. His only "friends" are the people he kills things with — and [[Linzi]]. *"Is that what women do with their friends? Go kill things? That doesn't sound right…"* He's genuinely lost.

**Astor's wisdom (mid-battle).** As the ambush begins — [[Lucas]]'s illusory fireball-and-feint scattering the trolls, arrows raining from the dark — Astor curses (the tea set was a gift from his in-laws; his wife will kill him if he loses it), grabs his horned helm, and delivers the line plainly: *"Friends aren't the people you take lives with. They're the people you share life with!"* He calls back to the rest of the party hidden behind them: *"Remember them in the moments to come!"*

**The charge.** A robed troll shaman raises a skull-topped staff and looses a fireball into the grouped archers — half vanish in illusory mist (Pavel chuckles that [[Lucas]] will brag for a week), the rest "flee" to bait the trolls and satyrs into a charge. Pavel leaps from his rock, transforming one of Astor's tea cups into "travel-sized shards" as he goes. *"The shaman is mine."* [[Lee]] sprints past laughing; [[Rindle (Construct)|Rindle]]'s engines whoosh behind. As they charge downhill together, Pavel keeps probing — *"Theoretically, if a woman said she respected you and didn't want to play games, and she liked Cat and wanted to be your friend, what would you do next? Theoretically."*

**"Serenity now."** Astor, worn thin by the questioning mid-charge, pulls a lever on his armor and vents an alchemical brew from its tank over himself — a rage-suppressant that distances him from his rising fury and shifts him to the perspective of *"a neutral observer."* (He notes Milo could maybe build a replica later.) His theoretical answer: *"I'd tell her I'd like to get to know her better. Maybe over a nice meal."* Pavel is horrified — *"You mean **talk** to her? What would I have to talk to her about?"*

**The counter-ambush.** The bait works too well. [[Nyrissa]]'s troops stop and pivot with startling **discipline** — satyrs draw pre-cranked crossbows and loose a volley. [[Lee]] dodges; bolts rattle off [[Rindle (Construct)|Rindle]]; [[Milo]] shouts. Pavel, lost in his own head, reacts too slowly — [[Astor]] throws his shield across to deflect the storm, taking hits on his own plate. Astor's save comes *nearly too late*, unusual for a man of his reflexes (he's rattled too). The trolls split: a bodyguard screens two shamans who rake [[Riven]] and [[Lucas]]'s hidden archers with sky-drawn lightning; the rest move to intercept Astor and Pavel.

**The Dance of Death.** Astor sends Pavel to help Lee and Milo — *"I'll catch up."* Pavel springs over the troll line, somersaults, and drops a figurine: **[[Cat]]** materializes with a bestial roar. Fighting as a pair, they are masters of the *Dance of Death* (Pavel's private counterpoint to the courtly *[[The Sword and the Suitor]]* he could never perform). Here, for a moment, his world makes sense: he is the **Warden**, chief defender of his pack. **[[Briar]]** flickers among the satyrs — drawing blood *and* keeping them perpetually confused — while Lee and Milo drop them.

**The Presence.** As Pavel strikes, a warm **Presence** swells in the back of his mind — the self-conscious pleasure of *having pleased someone and earned a smile*, evoking his mother and [[Edina Aldori|Edina]] at once. It pushes at his emotional centers, trying to form words in his head, purring like *"the fake purr… from women back in Nightmarch competing for a man's attention."* Feeling violated, Pavel shoves back at it — and his head fills with a memory of Edina's intent look and her parting line: *"Be careful. I don't want our new friendship to end just as it's getting started."* *(GM: this is an early stirring of [[Briar|Briara]] — see her note.)*

**Down, and back.** Gripped by indecision over Edina, Pavel fights distractedly — Astor twice shields him from troll axes (*"Get your head back in the fight, boy!"*), Lee finishes an ogre off his distraction — until a troll shaman's lightning bolt catches him square in the chest and his world goes black. He wakes to the icy chill of **[[Great Aunt Sumi]]'s** healing spirit and her familiar disapproval. Astor is beating back a shaman; [[Riven]]'s bow staggers the other; [[Lucas]] tends [[Lee]]'s wounded arm. *"This,"* Pavel thinks, *"is what friends are. Not whatever Edina meant."*

**The Ankou.** Then the enemy cheers: the darkness itself gathers into a thirty-foot figure of night and flame — black wings like a cloak, a ribbed chest leaking orange fire, razor claws, a spiked tail for a lower body. An **[[The Oaktop Ankou|Ankou]]**, a First World general — the mind behind the disciplined counter-ambush — emerging to rally its troops and test itself against the weakened Nightmarch leaders. Facing it, Pavel finds cold clarity: *"Edina wants to be friends. I don't know what that means — but more important, I don't think I want to know. I don't think I can handle it. Who would have guessed I'm such a coward?"* He hoists [[Briar]], points it at the Ankou, and launches himself at its flaming head. **Scene ends on the charge.**

## Notable Details / Flags

- Establishes **Astor's marriage backstory**: he opened with "let's be friends" to his now-wife because asking for more felt like too much. His tea set was a gift from his in-laws.
- **Astor's armor rage-brew** ("Serenity now"): an alchemical draught piped from a tank in his plate, vented by a lever, that suppresses his fury and gives him a "neutral observer" perspective. (He muses Milo could replicate it.)
- **Early Briar/Briara stirring** — while Pavel wields [[Briar]] in the fight, a warm *Presence* reaches into his mind (pleasure at "having pleased someone," echoes of his mother and Edina) and tries to speak. This predates Briar's full awakening (~Session 102) by a long way; flagged in [[Briar]].
- **Pavel is downed** by a troll shaman's lightning and revived by [[Great Aunt Sumi]] — another appearance of his ancestral healing spirit (cf. [[Pavel's Parentage]]).
- **New enemy — [[The Oaktop Ankou]]:** a First World Ankou commanded the disciplined counter-ambush. The RP cuts off on Pavel's charge; the party went on to **kill it** and break the garrison (defeated).
- **Pavel's private resolution:** he decides he's too afraid to face what [[Edina Aldori|Edina]] means and calls himself a coward — an emotional beat for [[Pavel and Edina]], even as he throws himself at the Ankou.
- Direct continuation of [[Pavel and Edina]] — Pavel is wrestling with what Edina's "friendship" actually means.
- Confirms [[Nyrissa]]'s forces (redcaps, trolls, satyrs, troll shamans) are strip-mining captured territory for silver — the [[Oaktop]] operation — and that the assault was **generaled by a First World Ankou**, not just monstrous rabble.
- **Images stripped:** the source ends with a Discord-hosted battle image (Pavel vs. the Ankou), not preserved in the vault.
