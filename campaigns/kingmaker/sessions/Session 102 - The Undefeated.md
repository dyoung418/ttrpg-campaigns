### Why the host exists (design note)
This party of six (plus [[Cat]], [[Bird]], and [[Rindle (Construct)]]) **dismantles solo enemies too easily**, even ones several levels higher. The host flips the action economy back toward a real fight, gives [[Lee]]/[[Pavel]]/[[Astor]] ground-level targets while the ranged work the dragon (so the kiting is *tense*, not feel-bad), and frames Ilthuliak perfectly: she keeps her vanquished as stone and wakes them to fight again — her "never lost" record made literal.

### The Frozen Vanguard — quick reference
**Full statblock: [[Glade J — The High Folly]].** In brief: Creature 15, **AC 40**, HP 200 + **Hardness 8**, **3d12+14** lance (~46 on a charge), Shatterstone burst on death/crit, **mindless (immune to [[Lucas]]'s fascination — but Ilthuliak isn't).**

**The levers that keep it hard *and* fair:**
- **Mindless → immune to fascination.** Last session that 9th-level fascinate *slowed the whole enemy line*; the host can't be captivated, so they're broken the hard way. **Ilthuliak is NOT immune** — Lucas's tool isn't dead, it's *aimed at the boss.*
- **Hardness 8** rewards big hits and crits; chip damage feels like nothing.
- **No-HP-kill levers:** Trip/Shove/grapple aren't mental — [[Astor]]/[[Pavel]] can shove a soldier into the **acid lake** or off a ledge to delete it; *reverse gravity* flings them too.

**Pacing (the host is a tap, not a fixed wave):** Ilthuliak's **Animate Troops** raises 3 soldiers and recharges in 1d4 rounds — she keeps 3+ on the party the whole fight, which is what stops six PCs from focus-firing her down. Read the table: drowning → let the recharge run long / "miss" a turn; cruising → she taps it every chance. **Killing her shuts off the supply — the host falls still when she dies.** That's the win condition; make sure the party can feel it.

*Boss tactics — [[Ilthuliak]] (her full block & spells live in her NPC note):*
- **Pre-combat** (she sees them coming): *time stop* (3 free rounds) → **Animate Troops** (opening batch of 3) → *disappearance* (invisible) + *spell turning* + *true seeing* → positions for the breath line. Re-tap Animate Troops whenever it recharges.
- **Round 1:** breath weapon — 22d6 acid, 100-ft line, DC 44 Reflex.
- **In combat:** circles overhead (kites — Lee's frustration); *reverse gravity*, *black tentacles*, *weird*, *horrid wilting*; her signature is **Grab-and-drop / *reverse gravity* over the acid lake** — also her reason to come into reach.
- **Resolve:** fights to the death — no retreat, no negotiation.
- **Bring-her-down levers (seed at least two, telegraphed):** climb a frozen lancer and leap onto her on a swoop (reward [[Lee]] with Panache); Athletics a statue down across her flight lane to force her low; [[Milo]] gadget-tether / [[Riven]] arrows as the ranged answer to "she's a dot in the sky"; **a fascinated Ilthuliak stops kiting and breathing** — Lucas's debuff *becomes* the window the melee needs.

> [!warning] This is genuinely above-extreme — six Creature-15 soldiers *plus* a Creature-21 dragon against six PCs. **Someone can actually die here.** Make sure the party is topped off coming out of the road beat, and keep the stagger lever in hand to breathe if it tips too far.
