# Session 105 — The Thing That Cannot Be Forgiven

> **Date**:
> **Status**: Planned
> **Previous Session**: [[Session 104 - The First Mercy]]

> [!note] Where this sits in the endgame
> Still the **run-up**, not the finale. The whole [[House at the Edge of Time]] is a **4–5 session
> arc**. Session 105's two jobs: **(1)** *finish* the mercy at the gate — grace, not discovery this time —
> and **(2)** deliver the **counter-lesson**: the courtyard [[#The Elite Sard (K2)|Sard]] is the first thing
> mercy *cannot touch.* After a whole night of forgiving, the party meets an enemy that is simply an enemy —
> the muscle they'll need when they reach [[The Wriggling Man]]. Don't rush toward the Queen; there's a whole
> House between here and her.

## The Endgame Spine *(carried from 104 — read this first)*

The House is a graduated **kill-or-forgive tutorial**, so mercy is a discovered, practiced option long
before the Queen — never a surprise rules-lookup at the final boss.

1. **The Gatehouse (104 → *finished this session*)** — the ashamed dead. *Some "monsters" here want release,
   not death.* The verb **forgive** is now known and practiced.
2. **The courtyard Sard (this session)** — the **counter-example in miniature**: a made guardian with no
   Shame. Mercy does nothing. *Some enemies you just fight.* This trains the muscle for #3.
3. **[[The Wriggling Man]] (mid-crawl, holds [[Lyriel]])** — the full counter-example. A deceiver and the
   [[The Lantern King|Lantern King]]'s spy; killing him is unambiguously right. The party learns to ask
   *which kind of enemy* they face.
4. **[[Lyriel]]'s rescue (mid-crawl)** — the thesis spoken aloud, by the healer who is the proof of Riven's
   own redemption. Happens *before* the Nyrissa confrontation so it can inform the choice.
5. **The capstone** — trapped captives (K19 Bythos Aeons, etc.) are *freed only if Nyrissa is killed **or**
   forgiven.* Either ending frees the House's prisoners.

### The Sard fight — the courtyard is its second front
A lone Creature 20 loses the action-economy war against six honed level-17 PCs (same problem as
[[Tarlaxian]]). The fix is **thematic**: the House fights *with* it. Run the courtyard as a **Complex Hazard**
that rolls initiative and acts on its own turn — attrition + debuff, not a second boss. Its Time-Flicker
**moves the Sard**, giving you the cinematic "it was over there, now it's on you" beat. *(Duplicate copy
lives in [[House at the Edge of Time]] K2 so it travels with the room.)*

> [!danger] The Court of Watching Faces — **Complex Hazard 19**
> **Traits** Complex · Environmental · Magical · Occult · Mental
> **Stealth** +33 (this is its initiative; the faces are obvious)
>
> **Disable** DC 45 Religion or Occultism (three successes; each quiets one face-cluster and removes one
> Routine action); **or** DC 43 Performance to out-sing the Mocking Chorus (until the Court's next turn);
> **or** shatter the faces directly (*Break the Faces*).
>
> **AC** 43; **Fort** +31, **Ref** +23, **Will** +35
> Individual faces: **AC 40, Hardness 20, HP 60 (BT 30)** each. The courtyard as a whole can't be destroyed.
> **Immunities** critical hits, object immunities, precision, unconscious *(not immune to mental)*
>
> **Constant — The House Watches** (aura, occult, mental) — the **Sard can't be flanked** here (a face slides
> to its back; never flat-footed to flanking). Hidden/undetected creatures gain **no circumstance bonus** vs.
> a House denizen.
> **Constant — Grief-Soaked Air** — a **frightened** creature in the courtyard **can't reduce its frightened
> value below 1** at end of turn. Ends on leaving or when the Court is disabled.
>
> **Routine (2 actions)** — on its initiative, in order:
> 1. **Wail of the Frightened Faces** ✦ (auditory, emotion, fear, mental) — each non-denizen in the courtyard
>    attempts **DC 43 Will**: *Success* unaffected · *Failure* frightened 1 · *Crit Failure* frightened 2.
> 2. **Mocking Chorus** ✦ (auditory, linguistic, mental) — one chosen creature takes **−2 circumstance** to
>    its next attack roll, skill check, or save before the Court's next turn.
>
> *(Each face-cluster destroyed or quieted removes one Routine action; when both are gone the Court is
> disabled for the fight.)*
>
> **Shifting Regard** ⤾ (reaction, once/round; **Trigger** a creature ends a move action adjacent to a wall
> or door) — a face lunges: **maw +33**, 4d8+15 mental (basic DC 43 Will), and on a hit **DC 43 Will** or
> frightened 1.
> **Time-Flicker** ⤳ (free action, once/encounter; **Trigger** the Sard begins its turn) — the sky lurches
> and the guardian slips between frames of time, gaining an extra **Stride or Step**.
> **The Queen's Eye** ⤾ (reaction; **Trigger** a creature uses Forgive Foe or [[Briar|Briara]] speaks in
> Nyrissa's name here) — the faces swivel toward the source. The on-screen tell for **Secret 3**: cues
> [[Lucas]]'s ancestors to warn him the Queen just *felt* it. (Optional teeth: that creature is **observed**,
> losing House concealment until its next turn.)
>
> **Break the Faces** — faces are destructible (AC 40, Hardness 20, HP 60, BT 30). Every **3** destroyed
> removes one Routine action — real work for a Guardian/striker who'd rather smash walls than the Sard.
> **Reset** — rebuilds when the House resets.

**Running the "mercy fails" beat:** let the party **waste one Forgive Foe** on the Sard — after a whole night
of mercy working, someone *will* reach for it, and it doing **nothing** is the visceral lesson. A Recall
Knowledge success then confirms: *"it's a made thing — there's no shame in it to reach."*

**Stragglers:** the 12 guards in the Servants' Wing (K15) stay **held back**, deeper in the House. Only the
five at the gate are in play tonight.
