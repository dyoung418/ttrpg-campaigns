# Pavel's Parentage

**Status**: Open — background mystery Pavel hasn't pursued

## Summary

[[Pavel]]'s mother claimed he was the natural son of the lord who ruled the castle above their town — born of a youthful love affair. The townspeople didn't believe her, and Pavel has his doubts. But when she died and left him with nothing, someone intervened: a stranger appeared at his door and offered him a position as a kennel boy at the castle. Pavel was 8 years old.

Someone cared enough to keep him off the streets. Pavel has never learned who sent that stranger, or why.

## The Questions

- **Was the lord really his father?** His mother believed it. The townspeople laughed. But the timing of Pavel's rescue after her death is hard to explain otherwise.
- **Who sent the stranger?** Did the lord himself arrange it? Did someone else at the castle know about Pavel? Is someone still keeping an eye on him?
- **Does the lord know what Pavel has become?** Pavel is now the Warden of [[Freehaven]] — one of the most powerful figures in the Stolen Lands. If the lord (or his heirs) are paying attention, they know this.
- **Does Pavel want to know?** He's never pursued it. It doesn't drive him. But it hasn't gone away either.

## Possible Developments

- **A letter from the castle** — congratulating the Warden of Freehaven, signed by someone who knows his full name is Pavelan Lebeda.
- **The lord is dead** — and his legitimate heir is contesting a will that mentions Pavel.
- **A half-sibling appears** — the lord had other children; one of them has heard the rumor and is curious, or threatened.
- **The lord is still alive** — and wants to meet the son he quietly watched over from a distance for decades.
- **It was never the lord** — the person who saved Pavel was someone else entirely; the lord connection is a red herring. Who really cared, and why?
