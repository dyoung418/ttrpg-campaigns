## Backstory Hook
Riven Sylvaranth, an elven ranger from the ancient city of [[Avennara]] in [[Kyonin]], was raised with a deep connection to nature and a sense of duty to protect the elven lands. After his parents were killed by demons, Riven swore an oath to continue their legacy. His skills and leadership earned him the rank of captain among the elven scouts, but his love for [[Lyriel]] led to a fateful mistake, resulting in the destruction of a village and his fall from grace. Stripped of his rank and consumed by guilt, Riven left Kyonin in search of redemption, joining a band of freedom fighters. However, the group's noble cause deteriorated into banditry, and Riven was forced to flee after defying orders to kill innocents. Now, as he wanders the [[Stolen Lands]], Riven seeks to atone for his past, grappling with the realization that true redemption lies not in great deeds, but in self-forgiveness and the relationships he has left.![[Riven and Lyriel.png]]
Riven Sylvaranth was born in the ancient elven city of Avennara within the lush, mystical forests of Kyonin. From a young age, he exhibited a daring spirit and an innate connection to the land, manifesting as powerful, uncontrolled primal magic that even surprised his parents, Aelir and Lytha, both skilled rangers. Raised on tales of his ancestors' bravery and the sacred duty to protect the elven lands, Riven was impetuous and brazen, often rushing into danger without hesitation. When his parents were killed in an ambush by a demon, Riven was devastated but swore an oath to carry on his family’s tradition of service to the land and its people. His daring adventures caught the attention of elven leaders, and he was selected to join the elven scouts.
![[young riven.png]]
As Riven grew in experience and skill, his prowess as a ranger and warrior became increasingly apparent. He spent countless hours mastering the art of the bow, using his father’s longbow [[Feywind]], his arrows finding their marks with deadly precision even in the thickest of underbrush or under the cover of night. His connection to the natural world deepened, allowing him to move through the forests of Kyonin like a shadow, unseen and unheard. Riven also honed his combat skills, learning to fight with both bow and sword, but he struggled to connect with others until he met [[Faelir]], a wise elven druid. With [[Faelir]]’s mentorship, Riven truly began to understand the depth of his powers. He learned to channel his immense natural power into spells and to temper his daring spirit with wisdom, maturing into a formidable protector of the land, driven by his oath. Recognizing his leadership potential, the elven commanders promoted Riven to the rank of captain, placing him in charge of a group of fifty elite elven scouts. Under his command, these scouts became one of the most effective units in the ongoing battles against the undead and demonic forces threatening the sacred forests of Kyonin. But Riven’s success in battle clashed with [[Faelir]]’s dedication to harmony, and over time their lives followed different paths, leading them to spend less time together.

Riven first met Lyriel on a misty morning deep within the forests of Kyonin, where the light filtered through the canopy like scattered diamonds. Lyriel, an elven maiden of ethereal beauty, had hair like spun gold that cascaded down her back in soft waves, and eyes the color of the deepest blue lake. She carried herself with a quiet grace, her presence as serene and calming as a gentle breeze through the trees. Lyriel was a reknowned artist and healer, known for her compassion and her deep connection to the natural world, which mirrored Riven's own. Their connection was instantaneous, two souls recognizing a kindred spirit in the other. As the years passed, their bond only grew stronger, a love as steadfast and enduring as the ancient trees surrounding them. They would steal moments together whenever Riven wasn't on duty, wandering through flower-filled meadows or sitting by the crystal-clear streams that ran through the forest. In the evenings, they would sit beneath the stars, Lyriel's laughter like music to Riven's ears, her gentle touch soothing the scars on his heart. They became each other's solace and strength, two souls entwined in a love that felt as eternal as the forest itself. The world seemed a little brighter, a little more magical, with Lyriel by Riven's side, and he cherished every moment they shared, knowing that she was his true north in a world often fraught with darkness.
![[lyriel.png]]
![[lyriel painting.png]]![[Riven and Lyriel - happy times.png]]
However, Riven’s deepening feelings for Lyriel also became his greatest vulnerability. While on a routine patrol, Riven couldn't resist the pull of his heart and diverted his force to visit Lyriel, longing for her presence and the solace she brought. He lingered longer than he should have, savoring every moment with her—the way her laughter filled the air, the warmth of her touch, and the way her golden hair caught the light as they strolled through the forest. But this tender moment of weakness led to a terrible oversight. Unbeknownst to Riven, the undead were advancing upon a nearby village. By the time he realized his grave mistake, it was too late. Riven led his force with a zealous fury, cutting through the undead ranks and driving them back with unmatched skill and determination. But despite his efforts, the village had already been overrun, its people slaughtered. The village lay in ruins, a testament to his failure to act when duty called. The blood of the innocent weighed heavily on his soul, and the realization that his love for Lyriel had caused him to falter and fail in his oath haunted him from that moment forward.
![[rivens failure.png]]

Riven stood before the elven council, his heart pounding with a mix of dread and guilt, as they pronounced their judgment. The court-martial was swift and unforgiving; he was publicly stripped of his rank, the once-proud insignia of a captain torn from his armor in front of his fellow soldiers. The sting of their disapproving glares was sharper than any blade, and the whispers of his failure echoed in his ears long after the proceedings ended. To be shamed in such a way, knowing he had failed his oath and let down not only his people but also the woman he loved, was a wound deeper than any he had ever suffered in battle. Lyriel, ever compassionate, did not see his actions in the same harsh light; she tried to comfort him, to remind him of the good he had done. But Riven couldn’t escape the suffocating weight of his own shame. The image of the destroyed village, the lives lost because of his moment of weakness, haunted him. The knowledge that he had failed his duty, that he had let his heart lead him astray, became a burden he couldn’t shake. Even Lyriel’s love couldn’t pierce through the cloud of self-loathing that settled over him, and the shame drove a wedge between them, as Riven withdrew further into himself, unable to face what he had become.![[riven elven council.png]]


Riven sank deeper into despair and misery, finding no escape from the overwhelming guilt that consumed him. Each day felt like a slow, torturous descent, with no light at the end of the tunnel. Lyriel, ever gentle and loving, did all she could to comfort him, trying to pull him out of the darkness that had taken hold of his heart. But no matter her efforts, Riven remained inconsolable, his mind trapped in a cycle of self-blame and regret. He could see no future for himself in the elven kingdom, only the shadow of his failure looming large over every corner of his life. With no hope of redemption in sight and the weight of his shame growing heavier by the day, he made the heart-wrenching decision to leave Kyonin. Lyriel pleaded with him to stay, her voice thick with tears and desperation, but Riven’s resolve was unyielding. He promised her that he would return, but even as the words left his lips, they felt hollow, uncertain. Their parting was filled with an incalculable sadness, a deep and aching grief that came from not knowing if they would ever see each other again. Lyriel gave him a parting gift, an [[campaigns/kingmaker/items/Lyriels Amulet|amulet]], a symbol of her love. As Riven walked away, leaving behind the life and love he had once cherished, the future stretched out before him as a bleak and unforgiving road, with no clear destination in sight.
![[riven and lyriel - parting.png]]

Riven journeyed northeast, leaving the familiar forests of Kyonin behind, his heart heavy with unresolved sorrow but driven by a desperate need to find redemption. However, his concept of redemption was still tied to external achievements—proving his worth through great deeds. The wild and untamed lands of the River Kingdoms called to him, a place where the rigid order of his homeland gave way to chaotic freedom and the chance to start anew. It was here in the [[Embeth Forest]] that he encountered a band of freedom fighters, [[The Few|"the Few"]], a group of hardened men and women dedicated to resisting the tyranny that plagued the region. Drawn to their cause and seeing in it a chance to atone for his past, Riven joined their ranks. His skills as a warrior, ranger, and his deep connection to nature quickly made him an invaluable member. He led daring raids against despotic lords, his arrows finding their marks with deadly precision, and his knowledge of the land turning the wilderness into an ally rather than an obstacle. Together, they struck fear into the hearts of petty tyrants, toppling oppressive regimes and bringing hope to the downtrodden. For a time, Riven found purpose in their cause, the thrill of the fight and the camaraderie of his fellow freedom fighters giving him a sense of belonging he had thought lost forever.
![[riven - hard times.png]]

The turning point came when the leader of the freedom fighters was killed, and [[Rowan Half Dark]], a ruthless and ambitious man, took his place. Under Rowan’s leadership, the group’s noble cause quickly deteriorated. What had once been a band of warriors fighting for justice became a band of common bandits, driven by greed and ruthlessness. Rowan led them down a dark path, targeting not just tyrants but anyone with wealth, blurring the line between freedom fighting and outright banditry. Before long, they were no longer champions of the oppressed but mercenaries for the very petty tyrants they once opposed, enforcing cruelty for coin. One tyrant that the Few aided was [[King Irovetti]] of Pitax. Riven, trapped by his skills and loyalty, found himself sinking deeper into a life of moral compromise, far from the redemption he had once sought.

When "the Few" were ordered to start killing innocent men, women, and children, it was a line too far for Riven. The thought of slaughtering the defenseless was more than he could bear, and he refused outright, his conscience rebelling against the orders. This defiance marked him as a traitor in [[Rowan Half Dark]]’s eyes, and a hit was ordered on his life. In the dead of night, an assassin came for Riven, but his years of training and instincts saved him. After a fierce struggle, Riven defeated the would-be killer, and, knowing there was no turning back, he confronted Rowan. In a tense and bitter exchange, the two clashed, and Riven, driven by a mix of fury and desperation, killed Rowan on the spot. He fled into the wilds, pursued by the bandits he once called friends and [[King Irovetti]]'s men. But they could not catch him; Riven’s skills as a ranger and his deep knowledge of the land allowed him to disappear into the wilderness. Rumors circulated that Rowan’s sister [[Talia]] now led [[The Few]] and sought revenge and King Irovetti had not forgotten him. Now, alone and hunted, he wandered, carrying the weight of his past and the uncertain hope of finding a new path.

Riven wandered further east through Mivon, his heart heavy with the weight of his past, yet driven by a restless need to find meaning and redemption. It was during these solitary travels that he encountered [[Amara]], a wandering priest of Sarenrae, the goddess of redemption and healing. Amara, seeing the pain in Riven's eyes, spoke to him of second chances, of the power to atone for one's sins and the hope of a new dawn after even the darkest night. These words touched something deep within Riven, rekindling a spark of hope he thought long extinguished. Yet, despite her wisdom, Riven remained focused on the idea that his redemption could only come from great deeds and victories—never recognizing that the true battle lay within himself. With renewed purpose, he continued his journey northeast through the vast untamed wilds of the Stolen Lands, where he found an unlikely companion in an orphaned young grizzly bear cub he named [[Brumble]]. The two formed a bond, traveling together through the dangerous, uncharted territories. Eventually, Riven's path led him to [[Restov]], where he was summoned by the famed swordlord [[Lady Jamandi Aldori]]. It was here that Riven saw an opportunity to fight for something greater, to perhaps, finally, redeem himself and fulfill his oath and his destiny.

Yet, deep down, Riven still hasn’t faced the hardest truth—that no quest will ever bring redemption. It is an internal process of forgiveness and self-acceptance. The true challenge for Riven lies in confronting his guilt and shame, and understanding that he must value himself and those he loves more than his quest for redemption. His relationships with himself, his friends, family and those he loves are his path to redemption. Until he makes this shift, his journey will remain unfinished, and the redemption he seeks will continue to elude him.

## Motivations

- To atone for the village destroyed while he lingered with Lyriel — a guilt he cannot shake
- To honor his parents' oath of service and protection (the foundation of everything)
- To prove his worth through great deeds — *he believes this is the path to redemption, but it isn't*
- To protect the innocent at any cost (why he killed Rowan; why he defied orders)
- To be worthy of returning to Lyriel — though he hasn't consciously named this yet

## Character Arc

**The External Arc**: Riven has built a kingdom — [[Freehaven]] — and rules it as its Ruler. He has done great things. And yet the redemption he's chasing hasn't arrived. It never does. No victory fills the hole.

**The Internal Arc (the real one)**: Riven must learn that redemption is not a destination reached through deeds. It is an internal process: self-forgiveness, accepting that he is more than his worst moment, and choosing to value his relationships — [[Lyriel]], [[Faelir]], his companions — over the performance of heroism.

The backstory explicitly states this: *"true redemption lies not in great deeds, but in self-forgiveness and the relationships he has left."*

**Key arc beats to explore:**
- The arrival of [[Talia]] or [[The Few]] — forcing him to confront his past publicly
- News from [[Avennara]] — Lyriel, Faelir, or the demon that killed his parents
- A moment where great deeds are not enough — where only accepting himself will do
- Confronting [[Lyriel]] and facing what he left behind
