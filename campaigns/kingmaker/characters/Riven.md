## Description

You catch sight of a figure ahead, half-hidden in the dappled shadows of the trees. It's an elf, but unlike any you've encountered before. He stands tall and lean, his muscular frame honed by years of hardship and survival. His messy black hair is cut short and shaved on the sides, revealing pointed ears that seem to catch every whisper of the wind. His skin is adorned with intricate, nature-themed tattoos that wind across his arms and neck, partially obscured by the dark leather and worn armor he wears. Scars, faint but telling, are etched into his skin, hinting at countless battles fought and survived. His eyes are sharp, scanning the surroundings with an intensity that leaves no doubt about his alertness. There is something both dangerous and commanding about him, a presence that speaks of a life lived on the edge, where every decision could mean the difference between life and death. Though alone, he moves with the confidence of someone who has long mastered his domain, the wilds seeming almost to bend to his will as he strides through them.
## Backstory Hook
Riven Sylvaranth, an elven ranger from the ancient city of [[Avennara]] in [[Kyonin]], was raised with a deep connection to nature and a sense of duty to protect the elven lands. After his parents were killed by demons, Riven swore an oath to continue their legacy. His skills and leadership earned him the rank of captain among the elven scouts, but his love for [[Lyriel]] led to a fateful mistake, resulting in the destruction of a village and his fall from grace. Stripped of his rank and consumed by guilt, Riven left Kyonin in search of redemption, joining a band of freedom fighters. However, the group's noble cause deteriorated into banditry, and Riven was forced to flee after defying orders to kill innocents. Now, as he wanders the [[Stolen Lands]], Riven seeks to atone for his past, grappling with the realization that true redemption lies not in great deeds, but in self-forgiveness and the relationships he has left.![[Riven and Lyriel.png]]
Riven Sylvaranth was born in the ancient elven city of Avennara within the lush, mystical forests of Kyonin. From a young age, he exhibited a daring spirit and an innate connection to the land, manifesting as powerful, uncontrolled primal magic that even surprised his parents, Aelir and Lytha, both skilled rangers. Raised on tales of his ancestors' bravery and the sacred duty to protect the elven lands, Riven was impetuous and brazen, often rushing into danger without hesitation. When his parents were killed in an ambush by a demon, Riven was devastated but swore an oath to carry on his family’s tradition of service to the land and its people. His daring adventures caught the attention of elven leaders, and he was selected to join the elven scouts.
![[young riven.png]]
As Riven grew in experience and skill, his prowess as a ranger and warrior became increasingly apparent. He spent countless hours mastering the art of the bow, using his father’s longbow [[Feywind]], his arrows finding their marks with deadly precision even in the thickest of underbrush or under the cover of night. His connection to the natural world deepened, allowing him to move through the forests of Kyonin like a shadow, unseen and unheard. Riven also honed his combat skills, learning to fight with both bow and sword, but he struggled to connect with others until he met [[Faelir]], a wise elven druid. With [[Faelir]]’s mentorship, Riven truly began to understand the depth of his powers. He learned to channel his immense natural power into spells and to temper his daring spirit with wisdom, maturing into a formidable protector of the land, driven by his oath. Recognizing his leadership potential, the elven commanders promoted Riven to the rank of captain, placing him in charge of a group of fifty elite elven scouts. Under his command, these scouts became one of the most effective units in the ongoing battles against the undead and demonic forces threatening the sacred forests of Kyonin. But Riven’s success in battle clashed with [[Faelir]]’s dedication to harmony, and over time their lives followed different paths, leading them to spend less time together.

Riven first met Lyriel on a misty morning deep within the forests of Kyonin, where the light filtered through the canopy like scattered diamonds. Lyriel, an elven maiden of ethereal beauty, had hair like spun gold that cascaded down her back in soft waves, and eyes the color of the deepest blue lake. She carried herself with a quiet grace, her presence as serene and calming as a gentle breeze through the trees. Lyriel was a reknowned artist and healer, known for her compassion and her deep connection to the natural world, which mirrored Riven's own. Their connection was instantaneous, two souls recognizing a kindred spirit in the other. As the years passed, their bond only grew stronger, a love as steadfast and enduring as the ancient trees surrounding them. They would steal moments together whenever Riven wasn't on duty, wandering through flower-filled meadows or sitting by the crystal-clear streams that ran through the forest. In the evenings, they would sit beneath the stars, Lyriel's laughter like music to Riven's ears, her gentle touch soothing the scars on his heart. They became each other's solace and strength, two souls entwined in a love that felt as eternal as the forest itself. The world seemed a little brighter, a little more magical, with Lyriel by Riven's side, and he cherished every moment they shared, knowing that she was his true north in a world often fraught with darkness.
![[lyriel.png]]
![[lyriel painting.png]]![[Riven and Lyriel - happy times.png]]
However, Riven’s deepening feelings for Lyriel also became his greatest vulnerability. While on a routine patrol, Riven couldn't resist the pull of his heart and diverted his force to visit Lyriel, longing for her presence and the solace she brought. He lingered longer than he should have, savoring every moment with her—the way her laughter filled the air, the warmth of her touch, and the way her golden hair caught the light as they strolled through the forest. But this tender moment of weakness led to a terrible oversight. Unbeknownst to Riven, the undead were advancing upon a nearby village. By the time he realized his grave mistake, it was too late. Riven led his force with a zealous fury, cutting through the undead ranks and driving them back with unmatched skill and determination. But despite his efforts, the village had already been overrun, its people slaughtered. The village lay in ruins, a testament to his failure to act when duty called. The blood of the innocent weighed heavily on his soul, and the realization that his love for Lyriel had caused him to falter and fail in his oath haunted him from that moment forward.
![[rivens failure.png]]

Riven stood before the elven council, his heart pounding with a mix of dread and guilt, as they pronounced their judgment. The court-martial was swift and unforgiving; he was publicly stripped of his rank, the once-proud insignia of a captain torn from his armor in front of his fellow soldiers. The sting of their disapproving glares was sharper than any blade, and the whispers of his failure echoed in his ears long after the proceedings ended. To be shamed in such a way, knowing he had failed his oath and let down not only his people but also the woman he loved, was a wound deeper than any he had ever suffered in battle. Lyriel, ever compassionate, did not see his actions in the same harsh light; she tried to comfort him, to remind him of the good he had done. But Riven couldn’t escape the suffocating weight of his own shame. The image of the destroyed village, the lives lost because of his moment of weakness, haunted him. The knowledge that he had failed his duty, that he had let his heart lead him astray, became a burden he couldn’t shake. Even Lyriel’s love couldn’t pierce through the cloud of self-loathing that settled over him, and the shame drove a wedge between them, as Riven withdrew further into himself, unable to face what he had become.![[riven elven council.png]]


Riven sank deeper into despair and misery, finding no escape from the overwhelming guilt that consumed him. Each day felt like a slow, torturous descent, with no light at the end of the tunnel. Lyriel, ever gentle and loving, did all she could to comfort him, trying to pull him out of the darkness that had taken hold of his heart. But no matter her efforts, Riven remained inconsolable, his mind trapped in a cycle of self-blame and regret. He could see no future for himself in the elven kingdom, only the shadow of his failure looming large over every corner of his life. With no hope of redemption in sight and the weight of his shame growing heavier by the day, he made the heart-wrenching decision to leave Kyonin. Lyriel pleaded with him to stay, her voice thick with tears and desperation, but Riven’s resolve was unyielding. He promised her that he would return, but even as the words left his lips, they felt hollow, uncertain. Their parting was filled with an incalculable sadness, a deep and aching grief that came from not knowing if they would ever see each other again. Lyriel gave him a parting gift, an [[campaigns/kingmaker/items/Lyriels Amulet|amulet]], a symbol of her love. As Riven walked away, leaving behind the life and love he had once cherished, the future stretched out before him as a bleak and unforgiving road, with no clear destination in sight.
![[riven and lyriel - parting.png]]

Riven journeyed northeast, leaving the familiar forests of Kyonin behind, his heart heavy with unresolved sorrow but driven by a desperate need to find redemption. However, his concept of redemption was still tied to external achievements—proving his worth through great deeds. The wild and untamed lands of the River Kingdoms called to him, a place where the rigid order of his homeland gave way to chaotic freedom and the chance to start anew. It was here in the [[Embeth Forest]] that he encountered a band of freedom fighters, [[The Few|"the Few"]], a group of hardened men and women dedicated to resisting the tyranny that plagued the region. Drawn to their cause and seeing in it a chance to atone for his past, Riven joined their ranks. His skills as a warrior, ranger, and his deep connection to nature quickly made him an invaluable member. He led daring raids against despotic lords, his arrows finding their marks with deadly precision, and his knowledge of the land turning the wilderness into an ally rather than an obstacle. Together, they struck fear into the hearts of petty tyrants, toppling oppressive regimes and bringing hope to the downtrodden. For a time, Riven found purpose in their cause, the thrill of the fight and the camaraderie of his fellow freedom fighters giving him a sense of belonging he had thought lost forever.
![[riven - hard times.png]]

The turning point came when the leader of the freedom fighters was killed, and [[Rowan Half Dark]], a ruthless and ambitious man, took his place. Under Rowan’s leadership, the group’s noble cause quickly deteriorated. What had once been a band of warriors fighting for justice became a band of common bandits, driven by greed and ruthlessness. Rowan led them down a dark path, targeting not just tyrants but anyone with wealth, blurring the line between freedom fighting and outright banditry. Before long, they were no longer champions of the oppressed but mercenaries for the very petty tyrants they once opposed, enforcing cruelty for coin. One tyrant that the Few aided was [[King Irovetti]] of Pitax. Riven, trapped by his skills and loyalty, found himself sinking deeper into a life of moral compromise, far from the redemption he had once sought.
![[riven-wanted.webp]]
When "the Few" were ordered to start killing innocent men, women, and children, it was a line too far for Riven. The thought of slaughtering the defenseless was more than he could bear, and he refused outright, his conscience rebelling against the orders. This defiance marked him as a traitor in [[Rowan Half Dark]]’s eyes, and a hit was ordered on his life. In the dead of night, an assassin came for Riven, but his years of training and instincts saved him. After a fierce struggle, Riven defeated the would-be killer, and, knowing there was no turning back, he confronted Rowan. In a tense and bitter exchange, the two clashed, and Riven, driven by a mix of fury and desperation, killed Rowan on the spot. He fled into the wilds, pursued by the bandits he once called friends and [[King Irovetti]]'s men. But they could not catch him; Riven’s skills as a ranger and his deep knowledge of the land allowed him to disappear into the wilderness. Rumors circulated that Rowan’s sister [[Talia]] now led [[The Few]] and sought revenge and King Irovetti had not forgotten him. Now, alone and hunted, he wandered, carrying the weight of his past and the uncertain hope of finding a new path.

Riven wandered further east through Mivon, his heart heavy with the weight of his past, yet driven by a restless need to find meaning and redemption. It was during these solitary travels that he encountered [[Amara]], a wandering priest of Sarenrae, the goddess of redemption and healing. Amara, seeing the pain in Riven's eyes, spoke to him of second chances, of the power to atone for one's sins and the hope of a new dawn after even the darkest night. These words touched something deep within Riven, rekindling a spark of hope he thought long extinguished. Yet, despite her wisdom, Riven remained focused on the idea that his redemption could only come from great deeds and victories—never recognizing that the true battle lay within himself. With renewed purpose, he continued his journey northeast through the vast untamed wilds of the Stolen Lands, where he found an unlikely companion in an orphaned young grizzly bear cub he named [[Brumble]]. The two formed a bond, traveling together through the dangerous, uncharted territories. Eventually, Riven's path led him to [[Restov]], where he was summoned by the famed swordlord [[Lady Jamandi Aldori]]. It was here that Riven saw an opportunity to fight for something greater, to perhaps, finally, redeem himself and fulfill his oath and his destiny.

Yet, deep down, Riven still hasn’t faced the hardest truth—that no quest will ever bring redemption. It is an internal process of forgiveness and self-acceptance. The true challenge for Riven lies in confronting his guilt and shame, and understanding that he must value himself and those he loves more than his quest for redemption. His relationships with himself, his friends, family and those he loves are his path to redemption. Until he makes this shift, his journey will remain unfinished, and the redemption he seeks will continue to elude him.

## Motivations

- To atone for the village destroyed while he lingered with Lyriel — a guilt he cannot shake
- To honor his parents' oath of service and protection (the foundation of everything)
- To prove his worth through great deeds — *he believes this is the path to redemption, but it isn't*
- To protect the innocent at any cost (why he killed Rowan; why he defied orders)
- To be worthy of returning to Lyriel — though he hasn't consciously named this yet

## Character Arc

**The External Arc**: Riven has built a kingdom — [[Freehaven]] — and rules it as its Ruler. He has done great things. And yet the redemption he's chasing hasn't arrived. It never does. No victory fills the hole.

**The Internal Arc (the real one)**: Riven must learn that redemption is not a destination reached through deeds. It is an internal process: self-forgiveness, accepting that he is more than his worst moment, and choosing to value his relationships — [[Lyriel]], [[Faelir]], his companions — over the performance of heroism.

The backstory explicitly states this: *"true redemption lies not in great deeds, but in self-forgiveness and the relationships he has left."*

**Key arc beats to explore:**
- The arrival of [[Talia]] or [[The Few]] — forcing him to confront his past publicly
- News from [[Avennara]] — Lyriel, Faelir, or the demon that killed his parents
- A moment where great deeds are not enough — where only accepting himself will do
- Confronting [[Lyriel]] and facing what he left behind

## Personality & Quirks

- Visibly tired from running [[Freehaven]] — the weight of governance is heavier than he imagined. He used to fight *against* people in power; now he is in power, which troubles him.
- Genuinely afraid he might be corruptible — he knows Nyrissa approached him, and he can't shake the thought that she did so because she saw something in him that resembled the [[The Stag Lord|Stag Lord]] or [[Hargulka]].
- Protective of [[Brumble]] in a way he doesn't fully voice; he worries the bear will die because of the trouble they get into.
- Gives compliments that he means seriously and is then surprised when the other person deflects. When he called [[Pavel]] "fearless and selfless," he meant it.
- His visceral anti-undead resolve comes from lived experience — 50+ years fighting [[Treerazer]]'s undead in Kyonin before he ever came to the Stolen Lands. When he says "never let corruption take root," he's speaking from hard personal history, not principle.
- Uses dark humor quietly — *"Reminds me of home"* about a cursed, mist-wreathed staircase. Understated, but there.

## Relationships

- [[Lyriel]] — former love; his feelings for her led to the fateful mistake that destroyed a village and stripped him of his rank; he left her with a promise to return; he has not returned; he carries her [[Lyriels Amulet|amulet]]
- [[Faelir]] — druid mentor who helped him find his powers; their paths diverged; Faelir may be the only person from Riven's past who saw him before the fall
- [[Aelir and Lytha]] — parents, deceased; their deaths shaped his oath; Aelir's bow [[Feywind]] is his most prized possession
- [[Amara]] — priest of Sarenrae who offered him hope in Mivon; her words planted a seed he hasn't fully grown into yet
- [[Talia]] — active threat; Rowan's sister, hunting him for killing Rowan
- [[Brumble]] — bear companion; found as an orphaned cub; one of the few relationships Riven didn't have to earn or apologize for

## Notable Moments

- **The Rise n Dine (with Pavel)**: [[Pavel]] rescued him from a table of young women (whether he needed rescuing is debatable) and bought beers. In the conversation that followed, Riven asked *"Do you think we can do better?"* — whether the party can govern differently from the tyrants they overthrew. He admitted: *"I used to fight against people like me... so many tyrants and despots."* He expressed worry that [[Nyrissa]] had come to him specifically, wondering if that meant she saw something corruptible in him. He called Pavel the wisest of all of them, and said the one thing he's sure of is that he's glad to have him along. He also mentioned his concern that [[Brumble]] might not survive the fights ahead.

- **The staircase at Vordakai's lair (with Lucas and Pavel)**: Riven connected the cursed mountain staircase to Kyonin — fighting [[Treerazer]]'s undead for fifty years in places "just as cursed as this." He named his purpose clearly: *"We must destroy it. Or chase it from this place. Never let it take root."* When [[Lucas]] pushed back with doubts about the group's luck and purpose, Riven admitted the Lyriel incident obliquely: *"I let myself get distracted by love just once — from my duty, from what mattered most."* He also stated a purpose shift: *"I came to these lands for my own reasons, but now... it's different. We fight for the right reasons — for this land, these people, and for each other."* When Lucas raised the Acts of State concern, Riven accepted it and asked for Lucas's counsel — a sign of genuine respect for Lucas as a peer.

- **The cave after the undead mage (with Lucas, Pavel, Benedin)**: When [[Lucas]] gave his origin story about [[Great Aunt Sumi]], Riven listened quietly and then opened up in return — the first time he told this to the group in depth. He explained his parents were rangers who died fighting [[Treerazer]]'s demons when he was a boy; that he served for decades and earned command; that he failed when an elven village was wiped out by undead because he missed it; that he lost his rank and wandered the [[River Kingdoms]], fell in with the wrong crowd, and came to the Stolen Lands with nothing. He found [[Brumble]] as an orphaned cub caught in a trap, saved him, and Brumble ended up saving him at his darkest time. Brumble is now in [[Nightmarch]] — Riven left him there because the path grew too dangerous; the bear has grown fat and happy. *"He's happy,"* said Riven, laughing. He also said, for the first time to any party member, that he hopes to return to Kyonin someday: *"There are things I need to do there."* Riven noticed the iridescent [[Lyriels Amulet|amulet]] slip from his tunic briefly and tucked it away.

- **The Great Feast of Freehaven (Nightmarch — kingdom celebration)**: Riven gave the opening speech, standing in a green cloak with [[Brumble]] sprawled beside the fire. [[Ekundayo]] nudged Brumble to roar and silence the hall. Riven moved through the crowd with practiced charm — sharing stories, showcasing artifacts, genuinely listening — the strain visible only to those who know him well; large crowds are as much a battle as a lich. In a quiet moment with [[Astor]], he disclosed that [[Nyrissa]] is behind everything — the Stag Lord, Hargulka, the Owlbear, the Cult of the Bloom, Vordakai — and said the fight isn't over. He then gave Astor a worn letter, sealed, addressed in flowing Elven script to **Lyriel Anarindë**: *"First, send this to Kyonin. I hope it's never read, but if it is, it might mend what I should have done myself."* He told Astor his second request: if he doesn't return, lead these people. He has already prepared official succession documents with the scribes. Later he spotted [[Cassandra]] (Silverstep Guard captain), spoke briefly with her, and offered [[Octavia]] the mayorship of Varnhold. He ended the night jumping onto a table to announce the **Grand Tournament of Freehaven** — five events, any team may enter, in three months. He also met quietly with the hooded guest (see [[Lady Jamandi Aldori]]) about a cautious alliance with Restov and received intelligence on Irovetti's background.

- **"I dare to hope again." (campfire at the Varnhold camp, with Pavel, Benedin — post-Vordakai)**: The party camped at the edge of a Varnholder refugee camp on the Nomen Heights steppes, a dozen fires in the hollow. [[Lucas]] lay still and pale, being nursed by [[Pavel]]. [[Feywind]]'s runes glowed faintly beside [[Riven]] as he poked the fire. He patted the bag holding [[Vordakai]]'s severed head with a small chuckle and said: *"I wasn't sure we'd survive that tomb, even with all our preparation. But here we are—alive, together. You lot… you're the best friends I could ask for."* He said he'd been avoiding thinking about the future — the risk of loss too distracting — but now he dared to hope again. He asked each of them: what do they hope for? What's next? He mentioned that at his pestering, a grove is being built in [[Nightmarch]], and that [[Astor]]'s mansion is finally finished (Astor might even let Pavel stretch out on the enormous couch). He said aloud that he misses Brumble.

- **"Maybe she'll come." (at Silverstep, with Linzi, Pavel, Benedin)**: Sitting by the shore of Lake Silverstep, [[Riven]] said quietly that the town reminded him of [[Avennara]] — the most at-home he'd felt since leaving Kyonin. He imagined [[Lyriel]] setting her easel on a specific knoll to paint the day away. And then, for the first time, he said it out loud to the group: *"Maybe I'll reach out to her. Maybe she'll come."* When [[Linzi]] pressed him, he pulled out [[Lyriels Amulet|Lyriel's amulet]] openly — described it as an exquisite rendering of a moon and a rose — and admitted that leaving was not duty but a foolish mistake. *"She was my anchor, my true north. I should have stayed by her side."* He asked Linzi for help: a letter, or should he go back to Kyonin? Linzi immediately offered to help him write it. This is the first time Riven has named Lyriel to the full group and the first time he has actively moved toward contacting her.

- **Morning tea on the Nomen Heights (with Xamanthe, Pavel, Lucas — post-Vordakai)**: At dawn after [[Vordakai]]'s defeat, Riven was already up and moving through camp with a kettle and easy confidence — entirely unlike the brooding weight he'd carried through the [[Tors of Levenies]]. He poured tea for [[Xamanthe]] and asked if centaurs race two-leggers, cheerfully putting his money on [[Pavel]] or [[Hyram]]. He teased, bantered, invited the group in. [[Pavel]] observed from nearby and remarked dryly to [[Cat]] that this resembled the Riven from when [[Nightmarch]] was small — distributing star berry pies, helping people build their houses. He wondered aloud if it was psychic trauma or psychological regression. Later, Riven gently asked Xamanthe how she ended up in Vordakai's tower and whether her herd would help confirm Vordakai was gone for good — the easy manner never dropped, but the leader underneath was visible.

- **"You made a mistake. Once." (with Pavel — at the tar pool)**: After the group crossed a tar pool one by one on [[Hyram]], Pavel stayed back to speak with Riven privately. He told Riven directly: *"Something about this situation has you thoroughly… well, 'spooked' isn't the right word, but it's eating at you. Are you fully here? Right now, in this moment?"* Riven admitted the Vordakai dungeon felt like the Tanglebriar — his parents' deaths, the comrades who fell, the burning village. *"I've failed before, and it cost innocent lives. I still hear the cries of the orphans."* He steadied. Later, when Pavel pressed him about why he couldn't return to Kyonin, Riven said he wasn't exiled — he left: *"I couldn't face my failure. Going back means confronting what I ran from. Someday, I will — on my terms."* Pavel answered: *"You made a mistake. Once. But you're not that person anymore."* Riven watched Pavel spider confidently across the cavern wall to the far side, nodded to himself, and awkwardly mounted [[Hyram]].

- **"Our bond is forged in trial, blood and the path of duty." (farewell campfire with Benedin — road from Drevlev)**: When [[Benedin]] announced his intention to return to [[Bronzeshield Hold]] and formally seek his people's approval to wear the crown, Riven nodded, eyes on the flames, and released him without hesitation. He named their shared accomplishments: saving Varnhold and the Nomen Centaurs, destroying Vordakai, stopping an army from overrunning [[Bronzeshield Hold]], catching an arsonist, freeing [[Fort Drevlev]] and stopping Armag Reborn. *"Our bond is forged in trial, blood and the path of duty. Now duty calls you to be with your people and you must answer."* Benedin responded by offering Riven something in return: a clear assessment of his growth. He named Riven's open-mindedness, his willingness to turn few away, his management of the culture clashes between frontiersmen, kobolds, and centaurs. *"Whenever I feel stubborn, I'll remember your open-mindedness."*

- **The Nomen Heights walk (with Pavel and Benedin)**: Traveling across the [[Nomen Heights]] steppes escorted by Nomen centaur warriors, Riven opened up unprompted — comparing the emptiness of Varnhold to an emptiness he feels inside himself. *"A thriving city on the outside, but empty inside."* He admitted he came to the Stolen Lands believing that doing enough great deeds would lift a terrible burden of guilt he carries from "long ago, from choices I made that can't be undone" — and that the deeds haven't worked. *"That guilt... it lingers. It's like a shadow that follows me, no matter how bright the day."* But then he had a breakthrough and smiled: *"I found hope in our friendship. You matter more to me than the adventures. And what we do to help the people of Freehaven matters more to me too. I think [[Amara]] was right."* This is the clearest statement yet that he is beginning to shift from external redemption-seeking toward something internal. He also expressed deep admiration for [[Aecora]] and the [[Nomen Centaurs]] — her fierce protectiveness of her people is something he clearly respects. Note: the centaur escort warriors overheard the whole conversation; their expressions shifted from stern disapproval to thoughtful appreciation.

- **Leading the armies (the Pitax War)**: At the final event of the [[Grand Tournament of Freehaven]], [[King Irovetti]] revealed his true plan: with all of Freehaven's rulers concentrated in one place, Pitax launched a coordinated military assault on multiple Freehaven cities simultaneously. The tournament was a trap. Riven stayed in [[Freehaven]] to command the kingdom's armies in the field while the rest of the party infiltrated [[Pitax]] directly and overthrew Irovetti from within — aided by [[Gaspere Liacenza]], a Pitaxian bard who defected. The war ended with Irovetti dead and Pitax absorbed into Freehaven.

- **The Silverstep Road (Pitax War — with Tovan)**: Riven's Rangers ambushed a Pitax supply convoy on the road through the pines — wagons heavy with grain, salted meat, and Irovetti's golden goblet livery. [[Feywind]] burned in his hands, fire and astral light curling with each shot; he moved through the fighting without haste, no wasted words, each arrow a breath released. After, he crouched before the surviving prisoners: mercy for the broken (double rations, flask to a wounded man's lips), arrows for the defiant. From a terrified driver, he extracted the intelligence he needed: the [[Golden Talons]] — two battalions — were marching on [[Silverstep]]. He said nothing to the prisoner. He rose. He turned to [[Tovan]]: *"They outnumber her two to one. We must reach her before they do."* The Rangers slipped back into the trees before the embers cooled, leaving no broken fern, no snapped branch. What Riven didn't know: a Pitax spy named [[Almon d'Avoine]] had survived the ambush by feigning death among the dead guards, and had heard everything.

- **[[Willowshade]] (Pitax War — Cassandra's last stand)**: Riven arrived at the pine-grove camp with thirty Rangers — he had sent [[Ekundayo]] north with the other Rangers to reinforce [[Serenko]]. Over a charcoal map with [[Cassandra]], [[Halvik]], and [[Senn]], he devised the Willowshade trap: forty halberds feigning strength across the meadow ford, giving ground slowly to draw [[Lord Darian]]'s two hundred Talons into the hollow where the ridges closed tight. Rangers on both heights. *"Mercenaries don't die for pride. They die when their paymaster falls and their captains lie in the dirt."* When Hadrik asked what if the Guard broke before the ford, Riven said: *"They won't."* Not loud, not forceful — certain. On the ridge next morning, he loosed arrow after arrow with [[Feywind]], each shaft trailing fire and pale light, and called the storm with his druid magic — hail, sleet, blizzard wind through the hollow. He killed [[Lord Darian]] with a flaming arrow through the breastplate. The Talons broke. Then he went down to [[Cassandra]]. She lay against the sergeant's arm, a spear through her ribs. He pressed his hand to the wound and tried to heal her twice. The green light guttered and died in the blood. She met his eyes: *"Don't."* He let the magic go. He told her she'd set the trap and won the day. *"Then… don't waste it,"* she said. Her hand slackened from the haft. At the temple of Desna he spoke over her: *"She spent her life on this field. We owe it to her and the others who fell today to live true and fight on."* That night he stood rigid, watching the funeral pyre drift out across the lake, eyes not on the bier but on the water and the mountains beyond.

- **Gold in the stands, hollow at the edges (Archery Contest — Grand Tournament)**: With six nations watching, [[Riven]] stood at the end of the line and won the Archery Contest with a common longbow — taking gold for Freehaven over delegations from Tymon, Gralton, Daggermark, Mivon, and Pitax. The champion of Gralton clasped his shoulder. A Mivon dignitary pressed a goblet into his hand. The coliseum erupted. And Riven scanned the stands, row by row, face by face, through every arrow and through the entire celebration. She wasn't there. The medal hung on his chest and the moment felt *hollow at the edges.* Later that night he stood on the outer wall above the celebrating city, watching the road.

- **"She wasn't there." (the outer wall — night of the Archery Contest)**: He didn't turn when [[Pavel]] approached. Just shifted and said it quietly, to no one in particular: *"She wasn't there. Didn't feel like drinking. Thought I'd watch the road instead."* Pavel stepped to his side, scanned his face — he's better now at reading the faces he knows well — and asked if there was reason to believe she might come. *"Hope's not much of a reason. But it kept me looking."* He explained: he'd written her a second letter, inviting her to come with the Kyonin delegation. He thought maybe he'd see her in the stands. First arrow, last arrow — it wouldn't matter. Pavel whispered back that he hasn't had that kind of hope in a long, long time, and then said plainly: *"If you need to go home to figure this out and make yourself whole… you won't go alone."* [[Astor]] arrived soon after — brought mugs, and when Riven waved off the drink, dropped a heavy coat on his hand instead. To Astor's question about what they were talking about, Riven answered in one word: *"Hope."* He mentioned Kyonin as home — Astor caught it and filed it silently, unsure what to do with it. When conversation shifted to the tournament, Riven refocused: noted Irovetti's cheating, authorized Pavel to go spy on Irovetti's subordinates, and sent Pavel off the wall without ceremony. *"The freedom of the people is at stake. We cannot fail them."* Pavel vaulted the parapet and dropped thirty feet. Gone.
