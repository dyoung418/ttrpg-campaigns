# Pavel

> **Player**: Darren
> **Class / Race**: Half-Elf (Aiuvarin heritage) — Rogue / Beastmaster, Druid, and Witch dedications
> **Background**: Field Medic
> **Level**: 17
> **Kingdom Role**: Warden of [[Freehaven]]
> **Full Name**: Pavelan Lebeda (he introduces himself as "just Pavel")

## Backstory Hook

Pavel's mother insisted his full name was Pavelan Lebeda and that he was the natural son of the lord who ruled the castle above their town — born of a youthful love affair. The townspeople snickered, and Pavel has his doubts. But something strange happened when his mother died: a stranger showed up at his door and rescued him from the streets, placing him as a kennel boy at the castle. Someone cared enough to intervene. Pavel has never found out who sent them, or why.

He lived with the hunting dogs from age 8 to 16. He liked them more than most people. At 16, he joined the army and served eight years in the scouting corps on the borders of the [[River Kingdoms]].

Near the end of his service, sitting alone in the dark one evening and feeling lonely, a large black panther walked out of the night and sat beside him. Pavel shared his food. [[Cat]] has followed him ever since. The army's regulations didn't accommodate a panther, so Pavel took the first chance to muster out. He wants to be his own boss for a while.

From his sheet: "Pavel's full name is Pavelan Lebeda.  At least, that's what his mother insisted on calling him, but he usually tells people he's "just Pavel," with no family name.

According to his mother, Pavel was the product of a youthful love affair between her and the lord from the castle above the town.  The townspeople snickered -- and Pavel has his doubts -- but it's undeniably strange that when she died, someone showed up at his door and saved him from the streets by giving him a position as a kennel boy, taking care of the hunting dogs at the castle.

Pavel lived with the dogs from age 8 to 16, and to tell the truth, he likes animals more than most people.  At age 16, he joined the army and served eight years in the scouting corps on the borders of the River Kingdoms. 

Near the end of his term in the army, he was sitting by himself in the dark one evening, feeling a little lonely, when a large black panther unexpectedly walked out of the dark and sat next to him.  Pavel shared his food with the cat, and Cat has followed him ever since.  This caused friction within the strict regulations of the army, so Pavel took the next opportunity to muster out.  He wants to be his own boss for a while.

Pavel doesn't follow politics much, but he considers himself a true child of Rostland.  If the Issians push too hard, he won't hesitate to take up arms again."

## Motivations

- Autonomy — he left the army specifically to stop answering to other people's rules; being Warden of [[Freehaven]] is as close to "his own boss" as he's found
- Loyalty to the people and animals he actually trusts — a short list, but absolute
- Rostlandic pride — he doesn't follow politics closely, but he is a true child of Rostland; if the Issians push too hard, he won't hesitate to take up arms
![[pavel-fire.png]]
## Character Arc

Pavel's arc is about trust and belonging. He prefers animals to people because animals are predictable — they don't lie, scheme, or disappoint in the ways people do. The question is whether the fellowship he's built through years of adventuring with the party has changed his calculus. Can people be relied on after all?

The other thread is his parentage. He was saved from the streets by someone, for some reason, and he's never learned why -- was it his father? That question doesn't drive him — but it sits at the edge of his awareness, the way a sound in the dark does.

## Backstory — Edina Aldori

Pavel has in-game history with [[Edina Aldori]]. Early in the campaign, the party was invited to a hunting competition held in dangerous territory — points for defeating creatures. The Aldori sisters (both nieces of [[Lady Jamandi Aldori]]) were also competing. The party came upon Edina and her sister in serious trouble with a stronger-than-expected beast. They fought together. They killed the creature. [[Jolia Aldori]] died from her wounds anyway.

Pavel was there through all of it. Afterward, he went looking for Edina in Restov — hoping for glimpses of her, moments with her — while telling himself she'd never notice someone like him, that he was never in her class. Her quiet *"Pavel? Are you okay?"* at the Great Feast, spoken by name, broke something open. Then he vomited on her boots.

## Personality & Quirks

- Quiet. He doesn't understand other people well and has a hard time with conversation.
- More comfortable with animals than with people — animals can be read and predicted; people cannot.
- Conceals his elven heritage. His hair is grown long enough to cover his pointed ears; he passes for human and doesn't advertise his half-elven blood.
- Not politically engaged, but his Rostlandic identity runs deep. The Issian/Rostlandic tension is something he'd act on if pushed.

## Notable Moments

- **The Kennel Disclosure (with Ekundayo)**: While trying to impress [[Ekundayo]], Pavel came up blank — humanoids remain unreadable to him. He scratched their dog Dog's ears instead and, unexpectedly, started talking. He mentioned working in a kennel from a young age after his mother died, sleeping in a room next to the kennel, and how much he'd loved those dogs. It was the first time he opened up like that in the party's presence.

- **"I never knew my father" (with Lucas)**: After being healed by [[Lucas]] following the Stag Lord battle, Pavel saw [[Great Aunt Sumi]] — Lucas's foxfolk ancestor — manifest briefly before Lucas's face. This cracked something open. He asked Lucas about his ancestors, and when Lucas offered to connect him with his father's family, Pavel revealed: *"I never knew my father. I never even knew if my mother was telling the truth or if she was a liar and a strumpet like everyone said."* He said he wanted to meet his father's family — but then threatened Lucas against using theatrics on him: *"I will escort to the grave anyone who takes me for a fool."* He stalked away. Cat stayed behind, tail twitching.

- **The Rise n Dine (with Riven)**: Saw [[Riven]] cornered by a table of young women at the [[Rise n Dine]] and interpreted this as needing rescue. Bought two beers, stood behind Riven scowling, and cleared the women out by sheer intimidation. Over those beers, Riven asked if they could do better than tyrants, and Pavel responded with an extended animal-kingdom analogy about leadership (lions, wolves, matriarchal herds) — eventually landing on *"Maybe the trick is to match 'em up right so it works."* Riven called him the wisest of all of them. Pavel deflected, and was hit by a Pavel Stick from behind. He added a tick to the chalkboard and bought more beers. Then he told Riven directly: *"You're no Stag Lord, and you're certainly no Hargulka. What you are is an elf who cares."* He then burped loudly and was mortified.

- **"We need to talk." / The ancestor vision (night camp)**: Pavel approached [[Lucas]] silently across a dark campfire — Cat by his side — and delivered a grimly simple opener: *"We need to talk. About Great Aunt Sumi."* After an extended staring contest (which never works on Pavel), he relayed Sumi's grievances: Lucas communes with her only for medical references, forgot her birthday, hasn't communed on Nine Tails or New Moon just to chat. She knows more than medicine — she's a renowned cook, could give dating advice to a kitsune bachelor. He argued her side of the thornleaf bark debate (steep it in hot water for 2 hours before drying and powdering; Lucas's otan root fixation is new-fangled). What Lucas gradually realized — and the Occultism roll couldn't tell him — was that this was not Pavel speaking. It was [[Great Uncle Olaf]], who had slipped into Pavel's form while Pavel's consciousness was somewhere else screaming in terror. [[Cat]] detected this immediately, grew twice her size, and her roar dissolved the possession. In the morning, Pavel sent [[Bird]] up to hunt, wandered to Lucas's fire, and found small tracks in the dirt — ferrets from a nearby burrow, he concluded. He also found Cat watching him intently until she was apparently satisfied he was truly himself.

- **"BLEARGHHHHHHHH" — The Sword and the Suitor (Great Feast, after Lady Jamandi slips away)**: Off duty, Pavel ordered a large ale and watched from a dark elevated corner. He observed [[Edina Aldori]] across the hall — noticing when her gaze seemed to drift toward his corner for a moment before returning to [[Astor]]'s arm-wrestling circle. He stayed. He drank. He watched Astor defeat a towering half-orc with theatrical effort and genuine victory. Internally: *I could beat Astor, though. Probably.* Then Edina entered the dance circle and performed **The Sword and the Suitor** using a **Pavel Stick** as the Sword, demonstrating mastery that Pavel found entrancing. She chose Astor as her Suitor. [[Linzi]] provided breathless commentary at his elbow. Pavel drank Linzi's drink. He watched Astor — who apparently knew this dance and used to be good at it — navigate the pattern and ultimately take the Sword from Edina's hand to thunderous cheers. Pavel's internal rage built across all of this: while he slept in a kennel, Astor learned dances and sword craft and social graces. Edina was from that same aristocratic world. Pavel was not and could not be. He knows he was never in her class. He assumed she didn't even remember the time they spent together before *her sister's tragic death*. He stood abruptly, sent a bench crashing, and started stalking toward Astor through a crowd that went silent and shrank back. Outside: [[Cat]]'s warning roar — cough-like, very large, very agitated. Pavel grimaced and kept going. Astor raised the Pavel Stick to a guard position. Then Edina stepped between them. In a quiet voice: *"Pavel? Are you okay?"* She knows his name. She remembers. The dam broke. He fell to his knees and vomited on her boots. Then buried his face in his hands. In the distance, Cat growled.

- **The Great Feast of Freehaven (guarding the hooded guest)**: Pavel's sole reason for attending was an assignment from [[Riven]]: guard not the hooded guest's person but their identity. He sat watchfully with his Pavel Stick leaning against his leg — ready for deployment against small talk, not just the chalkboard game. He reported to Riven on the guest's condition: he had tried explaining the variation in paw prints across ten jaguar species inhabiting the River Kingdoms; the guest had gone far away in her eyes partway through and never came back. *"Do you think she might be praying?"* Riven told him probably yes. Across the hall, [[Astor]] was effortlessly holding court — and Pavel's gaze flickered toward the group's direction at the sound of laughter. [[Riven]] noticed the briefest flicker of interest and clocked its true object: [[Edina Aldori]], visiting from Restov, trading verbal jabs with Astor. Pavel's mouth twitched in what Riven read as a grimace. Riven suggested he take Edina on a walk and tell her about the jaguars.

- **"No matter what Aunt Sumi says." (campfire at the Varnhold camp — post-Vordakai)**: When [[Riven]] asked what everyone hoped for next, Pavel was occupied squirting broth into [[Lucas]]'s unconscious mouth via a mechanical udder crafted by [[Milo]], then gently wiping the "dignified spillage" off Lucas's neck. His answers, in order: (1) Get Lucas back on his feet, *"no matter what [[Great Aunt Sumi|Aunt Sumi]] says."* (2) Spend time with the animals — he has combat techniques to try with [[Cat]], and he's worried about [[Bird]], who isn't thriving and is undersized for his age and species. (3) Visit [[Nightmarch]] and check in on the Scouts — *"not that they need me anymore."* He also expressed his views on the afterlife when [[Benedin]] asked: *"I doubt very much that any god would want me for anything good. I'd rather rest until I'm reborn with other cast-off souls in some future age. But when you've got an ego the size of a god's, it's not surprising if you start to think of mortals as playthings."*

- **"Maybe he's psychologically regressed to happier times." (with Cat, Riven, Xamanthe — post-Vordakai morning)**: Watching [[Riven]] serve tea and cheerfully challenge [[Xamanthe]] to a race, Pavel exchanged a look with [[Cat]]. He went through the diagnostic out loud: *"I checked him for head wounds after that last fight, and I didn't see any. They say sudden personality shifts can be a result of psychic trauma as well as physical trauma, though, so maybe it was something internal…"* He squinted at Riven and concluded: *"This reminds me of the way he used to act back when Nightmarch was small, helping everybody build their houses, distributing star berry pies."* His verdict: psychological regression to happier times. Cat yawned. Then Cat left entirely to go investigate something near [[Lucas]].

- **"You made a mistake. Once." (with Riven — at the tar pool)**: After the group crossed a tar pool one by one on [[Hyram]], Pavel stayed back to speak with Riven privately. He told Riven directly that something was eating at him — *"I respect a man's right to carry his own secrets, but we need you fully here."* Riven opened up about his parents dying to [[Treerazer]]'s demons, the burning village, the haunting sound of the orphans. Pavel grounded him: *"You made a mistake. Once. But you're not that person anymore."* When Riven mentioned his hope to return to Kyonin someday, Pavel asked if he'd been exiled — and then revealed something about himself: *"I've always suspected my mother was exiled from the homeland."* (What homeland and from what is unclear; Pavel did not elaborate.) Riven said he left voluntarily and will return on his own terms. Pavel, satisfied, shouldered his pack and spiders-crossed the cavern wall above the tar, his agility earning Riven's genuine admiration.

- **The staircase at Vordakai's lair (with Riven, Lucas, Benedin)**: When [[Lucas]] asked about end goals, Pavel responded with the clearest articulation yet of his philosophy: *"I know what I'm doing tomorrow: I'm going to kill some dead things, or I'm going to die. I'll worry about the day after that later."* He then stated his purpose: *"Every pack needs sentinels and defenders, and that's my role."* Cat nipped his hand mid-sentence, prompting him to correct himself: *"Sorry, I mean that's all of our role."* He then counted the full pack aloud: the six party members, Cat (7), [[Rindle]] — [[Milo]]'s construct, spotted standing motionless by the cliff face (8), [[Bird]] — feathers in the dark (9), Astor if he still counts (10), plus [[Brumble]] who's "gotten a little chubby." He noted the mountains won't protect Freehaven from what waits up the stairs — must root it out proactively. Asked Riven about Kyonin and Treerazer.

- **"This is what happens when you leave us to make our own decisions." (aftermath — Candlemere Island, Session 23 / RP23)**: After the party cleared a cult beneath [[Candlemere Island]], Pavel sat at the edge of the Star Hole — a pit in which the heavens are visible, stars wheeling slowly underfoot — with [[Cat]] curled up asleep beside him. His left hand rested on Cat's moving side; his right held [[Briar]] in his lap. *"That's twice now we've put our lives on the line for this sword. And the worst is yet to come, I think. It better be worth it."* When he sensed [[Riven]] approaching behind him, he spoke without turning: *"I hope you learned your lesson, Riven. This is what happens when you leave us to make our own decisions. We make a right mess of things."*

- **"When did it get so complicated?" — the Star Hole conversation (Candlemere / RP23)**: The opening barb gave way to the most emotionally raw conversation Pavel has had with [[Riven]]. He admitted the fight terrified him: [[Cat]] bled out and he had to seal him into his statue mid-flight; the powerful mage they trapped in a box *"died flailing away with his fist like a child,"* which felt wrong, *"like the gods were having a laugh at his expense."* The deeper wound: he used to fight only for survival and his pack, but now he carries worries. Facing death, he felt *ashamed* — that he'd let Riven down, that the people who depend on them would be left to [[Nyrissa]]: [[Linzi]], [[Astor]], [[Brumble]], [[Ekundayo]]… and, almost inaudibly, [[Edina Aldori|Edina]]. *"I don't feel right."* Riven told him: *"You feel as you should... Nothing is broken in you, Pavel. You have more to lose now. So hold fast to it."* Pavel, characteristically, *"understands less than half of what Riven says when he gets moody"* — but the conversation kept going.

- **"What about the whole half-breed thing?" — parentage and Kyonin (Candlemere / RP23)**: Drawn out by Riven's openness about [[Lyriel]] and [[Kyonin]], Pavel revealed something he'd never said aloud: his mother **left the elven homeland, [[Kyonin]]**, and he always assumed it was because of *him* — that she couldn't stay having borne a half-human son. He raised *"the whole half-breed thing"* as his worry about meeting Lyriel; Riven dismissed it (*"blood tells me very little... Who you are is"* the measure). Riven then revealed he **knew Pavel's mother's name** and her family, met her once or twice, and that *"she was not a woman easily driven by shame."* He offered to take Pavel to Kyonin to learn about his mother and family — *"You are my friend. You always will be."* Pavel ducked his head: *"Thank you."* See [[Pavel's Parentage]].

- **"What does it mean when a woman says she wants to be friends?" (with Astor, above Oaktop — RP25.1)**: Days after [[Edina Aldori|Edina]]'s courtyard confession (see [[Pavel and Edina]]), Pavel was supposed to be running an ambush on [[Nyrissa]]'s forces at the [[Oaktop]] silver mine — he'd already silenced the hilltop guards — but he could only brood about women. He asked the married [[Astor]] what it means when a woman says she wants to be *friends*. His own frame of reference was useless: his only friends are the people he kills things with, plus [[Linzi]]. *"Is that what women do with their friends? Go kill things? That doesn't sound right…"* As the battle erupted, Astor told him *"Friends aren't the people you take lives with. They're the people you share life with!"* Pavel leapt into the charge — *"The shaman is mine"* — transforming one of Astor's tea cups into "travel-sized shards" on the way, still angling for advice: *"Theoretically, if a woman said she respected you and didn't want to play games, and she liked Cat and wanted to be your friend, what would you do next? Theoretically."*

- **Pavel Stick**: There is an ongoing gag in [[Nightmarch]] where locals try to hit Pavel from behind with a stick. He has a tally on the chalkboard at the [[Rise n Dine]]. He takes this with good humor.

- **"It smells like industry." (farewell campfire with Benedin — road from Drevlev)**: When [[Benedin]] announced he was returning to [[Bronzeshield Hold]], Pavel looked up from hand-feeding Milo's pan-seared trout to [[Cat]] and asked — cautiously, as if the framing mattered — whether Benedin was leaving for good. He then tried to argue Benedin out of it using Hyrum. Specifically: he'd been to Bronzeshield Hold. It doesn't smell good. *"It smells like smoke, and leather, and metal polish, and forges. And sweaty dwarf. It smells like... industry. Industry and people."* He nodded emphatically as if this settled it. *"No, I don't think you — I don't think Hyrum would be happy there. You're going to have to stay with us."* He waited for Benedin to see reason. Benedin just looked at him. Pavel lowered his gaze. *"Why? You're part of us now."* Benedin met his eye and held it, encouraging him to finish the thought. Benedin told him he was right — he IS one of them, always will be — and gave him a parting lesson: cold iron for demons, silver for devils; he'd write it down, it'd be clearer.

- **The Nomen Heights walk (with Riven and Benedin)**: When [[Riven]] shared his sense of inner emptiness, Pavel responded with the most direct articulation yet of his own philosophy. He compared Riven's elven longevity to a burden — building up imaginings of the future that reality can't match — and contrasted it with his own outlook: *"I'm probably 25 or 26... anyway, I always figured I'd be dead by this age, so my life has been pretty amazing by comparison."* He extended this to self-judgment: *"You can't even judge yourself, not fairly or honestly at any rate, because you're always judging yourself against some imaginary version of yourself that you'll never live up to."* His joke about putting Riven on trial when they get back to Nightmarch fell flat. He then pushed more directly: *"When you spend too much time thinking, you get this sad look on your face and then you're grouchy as a crow defending its nest."* He offered help. After Riven's breakthrough speech about friendship, Pavel was shocked by the emotional swing — Cat slid to his side and pressed against his thigh, as if grounding him.

- **"I haven't had that hope for a long, long time." / The outer wall (night of the Archery Contest)**: Pavel found [[Riven]] on the outer wall, above the celebrating city, watching the road. He trod carefully and deliberately hit every loose board to signal his approach. Stepped to Riven's side. *"A solid tactical decision. I approve. But usually a Warden's job."* He's better now at reading faces he knows well, and he knows Riven very well. When Riven explained he'd watched the stands for Lyriel the whole contest — *"Hope's not much of a reason, but it kept me looking"* — Pavel stared into the night and into the past and said, very quietly: *"No. I haven't had that hope for a long, long time."* Then silence. Then: *"You've got a big empty spot inside you that Freehaven doesn't fill. I'm beginning to think maybe it will never fill it."* And finally: *"Riven. If you need to go home to figure this out and make yourself whole — you won't go alone."* [[Astor]] arrived. Tournament strategy followed. [[King Irovetti]] is attending, cheated at the archery contest, and his true plan is unknown. When Riven authorized a covert operation: *"You're still against torture, right?"* — dry, flat, deadpan. He was. Pavel offered to infiltrate Irovetti's tent, but suspected the man is too careful to leave anything written down. His plan: a subordinate's tent — less careful, more useful. He glanced at Astor. *"Do you want me to leave now? If I jump down from this wall and you return inside with Astor, it will give you plausible deniability."* Riven gave the word. Pavel put his hand on the parapet, vaulted over without jumping, dropped thirty feet, landed without a sound. Riven strained to find him. There was nothing to find.

- **Midnight Joust (Grand Tournament )**: Pavel is representing Freehaven in the final event, riding [[Cat]]. *"In a fair contest, I'd put my money on Cat eleven times in a tenday. The big question is how they'll cheat. Not 'if' — 'how.' After all, it's the last event."* [[Riven]] warned him: Pitax may use the joust as cover to target Pavel personally, not just unseat him. Pavel acknowledged and said nothing else.

- **"Are you saying you'd like to be... friends?" (with Edina, courtyard of the Old Fortress at Nightmarch — before the Latest Bloom, RP25)**: Checking the lashings on his pack one final time before the party marched (three days after [[Nyrissa]]'s forces hit a settlement to the south), [[Edina Aldori]] crossed the courtyard on a direct line and stopped a few feet away — new boots, no preamble. She apologized for the feast; he waved it off (he acted like an ass that night, he said, and hoped it would be forgiven). She thanked him for the hunting competition and for being beside her when [[Jolia Aldori]] died: *"You don't forget the person who makes sure you're not alone in a moment like that."* Then she stated it plainly: *"I think about you, Pavel. More than I have any ordinary reason to. I have for a long time."* Pavel went completely still. Then, suspicion: *"Did Astor put you up to this? I'm easy prey. Are you playing games with me?"* — pain and anger. [[Cat]] nipped his hand, then stepped between them and circled Edina's legs protectively, choosing Edina without ambiguity. After Edina pushed back ("I respect you too much to toy with you. Don't accuse me of insincerity"), Pavel's cheeks went red. *"Oh. Oh."* Then, almost inaudible: *"Are you saying you'd like to be ... friends?"* Cat growled and swiped at him. Edina: *"Yes, that is exactly what I'm saying."* She lifted his pack and held it for him; he obediently slipped his arms through; she tightened the front lashings the way he would have done. Hand on his shoulder: *"Be careful. I don't want our new friendship to end just as it's getting started."* She told Cat to take care of him and walked away. He hasn't had anyone help him put on a pack since he was a boy. He has learned, somewhere along the way, to hold a woman's gaze without flinching.
