# The Sword and the Suitor

A traditional courtly dance performed at celebrations, apparently well-known among Brevic and Restov nobility.

## Structure

**The Lady** performs the primary dance, holding a long object (traditionally a sword; novelty items work fine) low to the ground before bringing it up to a fencing *prima* position. She creates her own unique pattern of footwork and Sword strikes — the pattern is personal to each Lady and must be learned by the Suitor in real time.

**The Suitor** circles counter-clockwise around the Lady as she turns clockwise. He begins outside the reach of the Sword and must follow the tempo of her steps exactly while reading her pattern to anticipate Sword strikes. If he completes a circuit without errors and has pleased the Lady, he earns the right to step closer. If he breaks pattern or fails to avoid the Sword, he is struck — and must regroup without retreating permanently.

**The ending:** When the Sword is finally extended and held still, the Suitor reaches for it. If the Lady accepts him, she yields it. He takes it from her grip, pivots them both to face the crowd, and raises it. The dance is done.

## Notes

- The Lady chooses her Suitor from the crowd — the choice carries social weight
- The dance is as much about reading another person as it is about movement
- A Pavel Stick is apparently a functional substitute for a sword
- [[Edina Aldori]] demonstrated mastery of the Lady's role at the Great Feast of Freehaven; [[Astor]] showed he knew the Suitor's role well and had clearly practiced it before his "retirement" from court life
