# Kingmaker Campaign Summary

*Pathfinder 2e Kingmaker Adventure Path (Version 2.0)*

The *Pathfinder 2e Kingmaker* Adventure Path is an epic, open-world campaign where the player characters (PCs) transition from simple mercenaries into the rulers of a thriving nation. Beneath the surface of empire-building lies an ancient, tragic fey conspiracy spanning centuries.

---

## Plot Summary: The Rise and Peril of a Kingdom

### 1. Taming the Stolen Lands

The campaign begins with a charter from the city of Restov in the nation of Brevoy. The PCs are sent into the **Stolen Lands**—a lawless, monster-infested border region—to eliminate a notorious bandit leader known as the **Stag Lord**. Upon defeating him, the PCs are granted the right to establish their own independent barony.

### 2. The Seeds of a Nation

As the PCs build their capital and expand their territory, they face hostile monster tribes and strange magical anomalies. They soon encounter **The Bloom**—fey-infused curses that cause otherworldly monsters and deadly flora to violently burst forth into the mortal world, targeting the PCs' growing population.

### 3. Regional Rivalries and Ancient Horrors

As the barony grows into a recognized kingdom, it draws the attention of neighbors. The PCs must navigate a localized zombie apocalypse sparked by the ancient cyclops lich **Vordakai** and manage diplomatic tensions with **Pitax**, a rival neighboring kingdom ruled by the vain and antagonistic **King Castruccio Irovetti**.

### 4. The Final Harvest

After defeating Irovetti, the PCs discover that Pitax was merely a pawn. The true mastermind behind the Blooms, the historical instability of the Stolen Lands, and Irovetti's madness is revealed to be [[Nyrissa]]—a powerful nymph queen from the First World. The PCs must ultimately take the fight to her demiplane, the [[House at the Edge of Time]], to save their kingdom from being entirely erased from reality.

## Nyrissa

**Nyrissa** is the ultimate antagonist, but she is a deeply tragic figure whose cruelty is the direct result of a horrific punishment inflicted upon her by god-like entities.

- **Alignment**: Chaotic Evil (originally Chaotic Neutral/Good)
- **Role**: Nymph Queen, Seeker of the Apology, Mastermind of the Stolen Lands

### Her Backstory

Eons ago, Nyrissa was a Nymph Queen in the First World who sought to elevate herself to the status of the **Eldest**—the demigod-like rulers of the fey realm—by carving out her own grand domain, *Thousandbreaths*. The Eldest (chiefly the trickster deity the **Lantern King**) viewed this as unforgivable hubris. They sent the **Jabberwock** to devastate her realm, overthrew her, and passed a cruel ironic sentence.

### Her Punishment: The Apology

The Eldest tore away Nyrissa's capacity for love, empathy, and compassion, forging these stolen emotions into the artifact sword **[[Briar]]**. Without her heart, Nyrissa became cold, detached, and single-minded.

Her sentence: present the Eldest with **The Apology**—capture **1,000 mortal kingdoms**, compress them via fey ritual into grains of sand, and fill a magical chalice. Once full, her sentence is complete and her emotions returned.

### The Stolen Lands Trap

Nyrissa turned the Stolen Lands into a macabre "garden":

- **The Cycle of Ruin**: For centuries she has subtly encouraged mortals to settle and build kingdoms.
- **The Harvest**: Once a kingdom grows prosperous enough, she triggers The Bloom. The ritual tears the kingdom from the Material Plane, collapses it into a grain of sand, and leaves the land ruined for the next cycle.
- **The PCs' Role**: The PCs' kingdom is meant to be the **1,000th and final kingdom**. She has been watching, testing, and subtly manipulating threats to make their kingdom strong—a perfect, ripe fruit for her final harvest.

### The Duality of Briar

Nyrissa desperately wants to find *Briar* (which contains her capacity to love), yet also fears it—because it represents the only thing that can break her focus on her task. Depending on the PCs' choices, [[Briar]] can be used to slay her, or as a tool of redemption to restore her soul and turn her against the Eldest who cursed her.
