# The Starlight Staff

*[[Saint Flint|The Master]]'s staff — the means to call her back*

## Description

A silver shaft that sparkles with the gentle glow of starlight, capped by a crystalline sphere swirling with constantly shifting constellations. It is immediately striking — the kind of object that reads as sacred or significant to anyone who sees it.

[[Eri Malai]] carried it for the entire duration of his impersonation of his Master, treating it primarily as a symbol of office. He "hardly thought to use it at all."

## The Guiding Star

The staff is enchanted with the Guiding Star spell (Pathfinder 2e). The spell guides its caster to the place where it was cast — not to a person, not to a destination of their choosing, but to the location of the original casting.

[[Saint Flint|The Master]] deliberately cast Guiding Star on the staff before she left it with [[Eri Malai]], intending it as a recall mechanism: when the situation was safe, Eri Malai would cast the spell, and she would follow the guiding star back. She was waiting in the woods for days after the spell was finally cast.

[[Eri Malai]]'s error: he pointed the staff at a star in the night sky and said, *"That one. That one will take you home, Master."* He assumed it would guide her to him. It guided him to where the spell was originally cast — which turned out to be the woods where she was already waiting.

## Current Status

**Lost — whereabouts unknown.**

Saint Flint recovered the staff at the post-war reunion (she bopped [[Eri Malai]] on the head with it). At some point thereafter the staff returned to Eri's care — presumably Saint Flint gave it back to him for the Candlemere mission or for some other purpose.

During the party's assault on [[Candlemere Island]] (Session 23), Eri lost the staff in the chaos of combat. His first concern after the fight was finding it; his second was that Saint Flint is going to be *"SO upset."* [[Pavel]] noted that the man he pushed into the Star Hole wasn't holding a staff — making its location genuinely unclear.

The staff may have gone into the Star Hole (the pit from which stars are visible and into which things fall and don't return), or it may still be somewhere in the sanctum.
