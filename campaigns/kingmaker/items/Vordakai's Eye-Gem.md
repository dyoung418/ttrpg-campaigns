# Vordakai's Eye-Gem

*The extracted gem eye of the cyclops lich [[Vordakai]]; a powerful and dangerous artifact*

> **Status**: In the party's possession — wrapped in cloth, inside a satchel
> **Danger level**: High — the party has deliberately chosen not to use it

## Description

The singular gem eye taken from [[Vordakai]]'s skull after his defeat. It is wrapped in cloth inside a satchel carried by the party. Even through this wrapping, it exerts a subtle psychic pull — everyone near it has occasional unbidden thoughts of wanting to unwrap it and take a closer look. This compulsion appears to be inherent to the item itself.

## Known Properties

- Psychic draw — generates an urge to examine it in those nearby; the compulsion is noticeable but so far resistible
- Origin: cyclops lich; the eye of a creature who could perceive and harvest souls and who slept for over 100,000 years

## Why the Party Hasn't Used It

The combination of a felt psychic compulsion and unknown scope of power has made the party cautious. Something this old, from something this dangerous, is unlikely to be safe to simply handle or use without understanding what it does.

## GM Notes

- The passive compulsion at the campfire scene is a useful recurring pressure point — it doesn't have to do anything dramatic to stay present in the narrative
- What *does* it actually do? What did Vordakai use it for?
- Could it be connected to [[Benedin]]'s research into the *[[Eclipsed Incantations]]* — specifically the imprisonment ritual? An eye that can perceive souls + a ritual that can trap them forever is a dangerous combination
- Could be relevant to the Nyrissa endgame — Nyrissa's condition involves her soul being severed; Vordakai harvested souls; the eye saw things ordinary sight cannot
- If someone were to use it — willingly or under compulsion — that would be a significant story moment

## Session History

- Recovered from [[Vordakai]]'s lair after his defeat
- Present at the post-Vordakai campfire; the entire party felt the pull to examine it; no one acted on it
