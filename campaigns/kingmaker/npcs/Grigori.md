# Grigori

> **Role**: Propagandist; orator; Pitaxian saboteur
> **Location**: [[Nightmarch]] (formerly)
> **Status**: Exiled/eliminated by the PCs

## Description

*Appearance not yet recorded.*

A charismatic and gifted public speaker — the kind of man who can walk into a room full of strangers and leave with half of them convinced they've been wronged.

## Personality

Intelligent, calculating, and genuinely skilled at reading crowds. He has a talent for identifying the people who lost something in a transaction and making them feel robbed. He stays just inside the letter of the law at all times — not out of cowardice, but because it's far more effective. A martyr made by the ruling power would double his supporters overnight.

## Background

One of the first serious internal threats [[Freehaven]] faced. Grigori arrived in [[Nightmarch]] while the PCs were away adventuring and immediately began delivering public speeches criticizing and denigrating their leadership. As the days wore on, he built a growing wellspring of support among disaffected settlers — those who felt they'd gotten the short end of one of the kingdom's decisions.

His method was precise: every governance decision creates winners and losers. Grigori ignored the winners entirely and found the losers — the family whose expansion petition was denied when the PCs built a different city block, the merchant disadvantaged by a trade ruling, anyone whose outcome was negative — and organized them. He spun narratives that cast each event in the worst possible light, giving the aggrieved a villain (their own rulers) and a community of shared grievance.

He took deliberate and careful pains to avoid breaking any of Freehaven's laws, ensuring the PCs had no legal justification to arrest him. Doing so would have meant silencing a man who had broken no laws — which would have validated every word he'd said about them.

He was ultimately exiled or eliminated by the PCs. [[Riven]] later cited him at the [[Grand Tournament of Freehaven]] as a standing reason why Pitax must be watched carefully.

## The Deception (GM Knowledge)

Grigori is an agent of [[Pitax]] — but he doesn't know it. [[King Irovetti]] used layers of intermediaries to hire him, specifically to maintain plausible deniability. As far as Grigori is aware, he was contracted by agents from [[Fort Drevlev]] to the west, not by Pitax's ruler. He believes he's causing trouble for a petty fledgling barony on behalf of a regional rival, not that he's a piece in a larger strategic operation.

## Relationships

- [[King Irovetti]] — his true employer, operating through cutouts; Grigori doesn't know this
- [[Hannis Drevlev]] / [[Fort Drevlev]] — who Grigori believes hired him; the cover story Irovetti arranged
- [[Pitax]] — the faction ultimately behind his mission
- [[Freehaven]] — the kingdom he infiltrated and destabilized
