# Lyriel

> **Role**: Riven's former love; the reason for his fall from grace
> **Location**: Kyonin (presumably — details unknown)
> **Status**: Unknown

## Description

![[lyriel 1.png]]
![[lyriel painting 1.png]]
![[Riven and Lyriel.png]]

An elven woman of quiet grace and clear attention. When Riven found her in her garden she was painting birches — simple cream gown with green at the hem, sleeves drawn back from her wrists, a loose braid pinned up with strands escaped against her neck, a streak of paint along one finger. She carries herself with the ease of someone who has long since stopped arranging herself around other people's expectations.

Her attention, when she gives it, is complete — the look she gives to unfinished work, to injured creatures, to foolish young captains.

## Background

A renowned elven artist and healer in [[Kyonin]], known for her compassion and her deep connection to the natural world. She and [[Riven]] met on a misty morning in the forests of Kyonin — their connection was instantaneous. Over the years their bond deepened into a love "as steadfast and enduring as the ancient trees."

She was entirely innocent of Riven's failure. He diverted his patrol to visit her; she had no knowledge of the undead advance he missed. When Riven was court-martialed and consumed by guilt, Lyriel tried to comfort him — she never saw his actions in the same harsh light the elven council did. But her love couldn't pierce his shame, and he withdrew from her as he withdrew from everything.

When Riven told her he was leaving [[Kyonin]], Lyriel pleaded with him to stay. He promised to return. She gave him her [[Lyriels Amulet|amulet]] as a parting gift. He walked away into an uncertain future.

She carries the grief of the village incident too — because he came to see *her*, and because of that, people died. She may blame herself for something that was entirely Riven's choice.

## The Reunion in Kyonin

During Riven's three-month diplomatic mission to Kyonin (see [[Freehaven-Kyonin Treaty]]), he found her house at the edge of the city where the gardens meet the wood. [[Thaelirian]] gave him directions. She was painting birches when he came through the gate.

She did not shut the gate. She said: *"You are intruding. But not unwelcome."*

He told her the truth — that he loved her, that he left, that shame had mastered him, that he had mistaken self-punishment for honor. She told him she would have stayed. She said she did not know what she chose now. She spoke of her own years without him: she had been angry in ways that were *"wonderfully practical"* — she gave the anger errands. Then the errands ended and she had to decide whether she was waiting for him or keeping faith with the girl she had been when he left.

*"I loved you. I do not say that as accusation. I loved you. Some part of that has survived, though not unchanged, and not obediently. But love is not trust. It is not a life."*

They agreed to speak again — she told him to come the day after, not the next day: she wanted to paint well and not have her mind on him. He returned the following evening. She stopped him giving a report on Freehaven: *"I did not ask for a report. Tell me what it is like."* He told her about Nightmarch after rain, about farmers who brought food to neighbors before being asked, about children daring one another to touch Brumble's paw. He told her about his friends — their tempers, their habits, the ways they had saved him and refused to become the kind of companions who could be praised simply. She listened with more than politeness. *"You love it,"* she said. *"I do."* She looked toward the birches: *"Good."*

He invited her to come to Freehaven. She did not answer at once. He added that even if not at his side, Silverstep at sunrise was worth the road. She said she was *"not saying no."* She asked him to write — ordinary letters, not formal ones. Which flowers come first in spring. What the council argues about. Whether the bear is real. He said he would write. She said she might answer.

After he left Kyonin, he wrote. She answered. In time, she decided to come.

Later, at the Star Hole beneath [[Candlemere Island]] (see [[RP 23 — Deep Under Candlemere]]), [[Riven]] spoke of her to [[Pavel]] with bare hope: he had gone to her only after he *"stopped trying to become the man who deserved to be loved,"* offering himself *"faults and all."* He said he longed to show her the sunrise over [[Silverstep]] Lake, and that he'd written to her about all his companions — *"she has heard a great deal about all of you. Especially about brave Pavel."* He promised Pavel the party would meet her. (This conversation predates her abduction.)

## Current Status

**Captive in the [[House at the Edge of Time]].** She answered Riven's invitation and traveled to [[Nightmarch]] — and was abducted upon arrival by a fey and trolls (the fey was [[The Wriggling Man]] in disguise, acting on [[Nyrissa]]'s orders). In the struggle she dropped [[Riven's Letter to Lyriel|Riven's letter]], which was found by witnesses and reported to [[Linzi]], who sent Riven a Sending mid-bloom. [[Nyrissa]] holds her as leverage — alive, not as a trophy. [[Nyrissa]] knew she was coming because she had been mining Riven's dreams.
