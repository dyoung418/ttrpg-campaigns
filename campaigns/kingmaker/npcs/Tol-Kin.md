# Tol-Kin

> **Role**: Wandering bard; one of the greatest living
> **Location**: The road — periodically visiting [[Nightmarch]]
> **Status**: Alive

## Description

An old human man who hobbles in leaning on a staff, stooped and quavery-voiced. When he performs, he stands straight, his voice rings clear, and the stoop vanishes entirely. He climbed on top of a low table to tell his tale at the Great Feast and held several hundred people in absolute silence for a full minute after it ended.

## Personality

Blunt and perceptive — he told [[Lucas]] his story was rushed before Lucas even knew who he was. Holds firm opinions about what it means to be a bard: *"Never trust a Bard who hasn't any scars or who's happy settling down. You don't collect stories in a tavern, not the good ones anyway."* Warm underneath the gruffness — laughed a deep belly laugh when Lucas immediately volunteered to join him on the road.

He knows the value of silence. After "The Halfling, The Dragon, and the Baker's Dozen Dwarves," he let the crowd sit in total quiet for a full minute before bowing. The applause after was raucous.

## Background

Has been around for decades and knows esoterica and stories from all over the world. He came to the Great Feast of Freehaven not by his own initiative, but because of a request from a trainee of one of his former students — [[Linzi]], whose teacher was [[Beneford]], who studied under Tol-Kin.

He offered [[Lucas]] a month of mentorship — to travel together, seek story-worthy adventures, and perhaps explore a rumor he'd heard about a specific location — and Lucas accepted on the spot.

He was interested enough in the announced [[Grand Tournament of Freehaven]] that he suggested the **Battle of the Bards** event might bring him back for long enough to tell his three-night epic *Frohdeaux the Nine-Fingered and the Ring of Doom*.

## Known Stories

- **"The Halfling, The Dragon, and the Baker's Dozen Dwarves"** — performed at the Great Feast; complete with magical music from no mortal instrument, illusions, and lights; the crowd sat in stunned silence for a minute after it ended
- **Frohdeaux the Nine-Fingered and the Ring of Doom** — a three-night epic; considered a true masterwork; he won't tell it unless there's time to tell it properly

## Relationships

- [[Beneford]] — former student; Beneford is now Court Bard for a noble family in the East; Tol-Kin considers settling down a creative death
- [[Linzi]] — trainee of Beneford; Tol-Kin came to the feast partly at her request; she traveled with him and Lucas for a month
- [[Lucas]] — saw his raw talent; accepted him as a month-long pupil; departed Nightmarch midday after the feast; hinted at a rumor he wanted to investigate as a group

## GM Notes

- His month with Lucas and Linzi is what triggered Lucas's Bard Dedication retraining. Whatever adventure they had offscreen during that month is deliberately unspecified — the GM can flesh it out or leave it as backstory.
- The rumor he mentioned investigating is undefined — a good hook for a future side story or as flavor for why Lucas gained Bard Dedication.
- He is likely to return for the Grand Tournament's Battle of the Bards event; treat him as a reliable (if unpredictable) future NPC presence.
- His assessment of Lucas: genuine talent, but rushes to the exciting part; has a life worthy of being a tale.
