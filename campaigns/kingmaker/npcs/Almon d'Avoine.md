# Almon d'Avoine

> **Role**: Pitax intelligence operative
> **Location**: Unknown
> **Faction**: [[Pitax]] (formerly — [[King Irovetti|Irovetti]]'s intelligence service)
> **Status**: Unknown — survived the war; whereabouts unknown

## Description

Unremarkable by design. Nothing about Almon d'Avoine invites a second look — his face, smeared with soot and mud, becomes anyone who crawled out of a battlefield. He moves without urgency and leaves no impression. He installed a false floorboard in a supply wagon himself: a man who plans ahead and trusts no one else's preparation.

## Personality

Supremely analytical. While mercenaries fought and died in the ambush, Almon was calculating the primary lines of fire from the angle of incoming arrows. He doesn't reach for a sword when he can't see his enemy — *"fools fought, but clever men hid."* Cold, patient, and utterly unsentimental. His disguise (dead guard's cloak, helmet with a convincingly broken arrow through the eye-slit) was improvised in seconds and held under scrutiny. He waited until the birdsong returned before moving.

## Background

Embedded as cover in a [[Pitax]] supply convoy during the war — likely running intelligence or escorting something of value. He survived [[Riven]]'s Ranger ambush by feigning death with perfect composure. In the aftermath, he overheard two critical pieces of intelligence:

1. The [[Golden Talons]] (two battalions) are marching on [[Silverstep]].
2. The Rangers' commander is racing to protect someone named **Cassandra**, who outnumbers his force two to one.

**Cassandra** is now Almon's target.

## GM Notes

> [!secret] GM Only
> Almon survived the Pitax War's conclusion with this intelligence intact. He may be working independently, for a Pitax successor, or for another power entirely. The party does not know he exists. He knows [[Cassandra]]'s name and strategic significance — he overheard it from [[Riven]] himself.
>
> His key advantage: he is a face the party has never seen, in a theater they didn't know was watched.
