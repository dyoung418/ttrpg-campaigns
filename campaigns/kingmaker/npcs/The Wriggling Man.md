# The Wriggling Man

**Creature 21** | Unique, CE, Medium, Aberration, Swarm (Variant male worm that walks wizard)

[[Nyrissa]]'s arcane advisor. A worm that walks — a swarm of worms inhabiting the shape of a man. He died long ago on a failed mission to the First World and was reborn as this abomination. His aid has been instrumental in helping Nyrissa manipulate hundreds of kingdoms across worlds. He normally lives in **the Mire** (Glade G) but is currently in the **Throne Room (K17)**  ([[Chapter 10 - Sound of a Thousand Screams|Chapter 10]]) of the [[House at the Edge of Time]].

## Stats

- **AC** 47 (all-around vision) | **HP** 325, fast healing 20
- **Fort** +34, **Ref** +39, **Will** +35; +4 status vs. mental
- **Immunities**: disease, paralyzed, poison, precision, swarm mind, unconscious
- **Weaknesses**: area damage 20, splash damage 20
- **Resistances**: fire 10, physical 20
- **Perception** +35; darkvision, tremorsense (imprecise) 30 ft
- **Languages**: Abyssal, Aklo, Common, Draconic, Sylvan, Terran, Undercommon
- **Skills**: Acrobatics +39, Arcana +41, Deception +39, Nature +37, Society +41, Stealth +39

**Items**: Bag of holding IV (containing full spellbook library), +2 greater fire resistant greater resilient leather armor, major staff of transmutation

## Key Spells

10th *time stop*; 9th *disjunction*, *magic missile*, *project image*; 8th *contingency* (already cast: dimension door to M6 at ≤50 HP), *illusory scene*, *mind blank*; 7th *reverse gravity*, *fireball*, *veil*; 6th *baleful polymorph*, *feeblemind*, *true seeing*; 5th *black tentacles*, *dimension door*, *mind probe*; 4th *fly*, *invisibility*, *suggestion*; 3rd *blindness*, *earthbind*, *slow*

**Quickened Casting** (1/day): Reduce cast time of ≤8th-level spell by 1 action.

**Effortless Concentration**: Free action to sustain a spell at start of each turn.

**Discorporate**: As worm that walks (can spread out as a swarm to pass through spaces).

**Squirming Embrace** (1 action): 8d8 damage, DC 44 Reflex. Only if he's already in melee (rare).

## Encounter Redesign — The Two Lyriels *(campaign version — use this)*

> [!warning] GM Only
> This **supersedes the vanilla "angel ruse"** below (kept for reference). The Wriggling Man is the
> endgame's **no-mercy foil** — a pure deceiver with *no Shame entry*, the counter-example that teaches
> the party which enemies to forgive and which to simply kill. His whole fight is built so that his
> **illusions make *rescuing [[Lyriel]]* the hard problem**, not surviving him. And the skill it forces
> — *seeing past a monstrous illusion to the wounded real person beneath* — is exactly what the
> [[Nyrissa]] choice will demand. He is holding Lyriel on Nyrissa's orders (leverage; Nyrissa mined
> [[Riven]]'s dreams to know she was coming).

### The setup (before the PCs arrive)
- **The decoy Lyriel** — an *illusory scene* + *project image* figure of Lyriel, chained dramatically
  at a **focal point** (the throne, or center-stage on the dais), crying out to [[Riven]] by name in
  her own voice (illusory sound / *ventriloquism*). She is **bait**, positioned to pull the party —
  Riven especially — straight into the kill zone.
- **The real Lyriel** — hidden **elsewhere in the room**: bound and either **unconscious or under
  *silence***, and **veiled** to read as furniture, a statue, or tucked behind an *illusory scene*
  "wall." She makes no sound and is not where her voice seems to come from.
- **The Wriggling Man** — unseen: behind the throne, *invisible*, or **discorporated into the walls
  as worms**, sustaining every illusion for free via **Effortless Concentration**.

### The vise — why the illusion makes the rescue harder
1. **The bait draws blood.** Rushing the decoy pulls the party over his prepared ground —
   *black tentacles*, *reverse gravity* (a column yanking them to the ceiling), and *fireball* down
   the room's long axis. The emotional pull (a chained, screaming Lyriel) is the trap's trigger.
2. **The hostage is a shield against their best weapon.** A worm that walks has **weakness to area &
   splash damage 20** — the party's optimal answer. He positions the **real** Lyriel so that AoEs
   aimed at "the statues," the swarm, or the decoy **risk hitting her.** Either they don't AoE (and he
   tanks single-target all day behind fast healing 20), or they do — and gamble with her life.
3. **The voice lies.** Illusory sound throws *Lyriel's* cries to the **decoy's** location; the real
   Lyriel is silent. **Following the voice is following the lie.**
4. **He can wear her face** *(optional gut-punch)* — with *veil* he can make **himself** appear as a
   second Lyriel, or swap the decoy's location, so at the crux the party may face a "Lyriel" who is
   actually *him*. Someone has to be willing to strike her face. Use once, for the turn.

### The outs — finding the real Lyriel *(reward what the party already carries)*
- **[[Lyriels Amulet|Lyriel's amulet]] — the homing beacon** *(the clean, thematic answer)*: Riven has
  carried her moon-and-rose amulet the entire campaign. Near the real Lyriel it **warms and the motif
  glows**, indifferent to illusion — **love finds the true thing.** It points at *her*, not the decoy.
  This is Riven's beat: he stops trusting his eyes and trusts what he feels. *(If he's since passed the
  amulet on, whoever holds it gets the tug.)*
- **[[Cat]]'s scent** — Pavel's panther has **scent (imprecise)**; illusions have no smell. Cat can nose
  out the real, living Lyriel and the worm-swarm behind the walls. A non-magical out that spotlights
  [[Pavel]].
- **Disbelief & true senses** — Interacting with/studying the decoy grants a **Will save vs. his spell
  DC (44)** to disbelieve; *true seeing*, *see invisibility*, and Recall Knowledge (Arcana/Occultism)
  on the illusions all crack it.
- **[[Briar|Briara]]** — as Nyrissa's stolen *capacity to love* given voice, she can feel the difference
  between a **genuine** frightened, loving person and a hollow projection. She points, quietly, at the
  real one. *(And it costs her something to be this near the House and still choose to help — capture
  it.)*
- **Recall Knowledge on him** — reveals *worm that walks*, master illusionist, **no honor and no mercy
  in him**: everything he shows you is a lie, and he is safe to kill.

### The payoff
When the real Lyriel is freed, she delivers the **endgame thesis** (see Session 104's spine): she has
been a captive audience inside this grief-soaked House and, as a *healer*, names what she's sensed — a
queen who is **hollow, not hateful**: *"Some hurts can't be cut out. You can only forgive them."* The
rescue and the mercy-lesson are the same beat: the party had to **see past the monster to the wounded
real thing** to save her — the exact move the Nyrissa choice asks of them. **He, by contrast, gets no
mercy** — he's the lie you cut down. He fights to the death (contingency *dimension door* to M6, then
returns to finish it).

## Boss Modifications — Six-PC Solo Fixes *(campaign version — use with the redesign)*

> [!warning] GM Only — why these exist
> This party is **six PCs, all played well** — a lone Creature 21 loses the action-economy war on
> numbers the same way [[Tarlaxian]] did. These mods give the Wriggling Man a **second front**, and
> unlike Tarlaxian his second front is *thematic*: he is a **swarm** (he can split) and a **master
> illusionist** (you can't trust which body is real), and he's already **immune to precision damage**
> (your two biggest single-target dealers are blunted). Layer these on top of the statblock and the
> "Two Lyriels" redesign above. Every one has a **dial** — soften live if the party is drowning, harden
> if they're cruising.

### 1. The Shell Game *(core action-economy fix — the illusionist's version of "Submerge")*
He never presents as one target. Going into the fight he is **three bodies**:
- **The true core** — the real worm-swarm in a *veil*. Only this one has his HP and can be meaningfully
  hurt. Acts on his initiative.
- **A spun-off swarm shard** — a genuine lesser worm-swarm (treat as a **worm-swarm add**, see #2) that
  **acts on initiative − 10.** This is the load-bearing extra turn — a second threat every round.
- **A *project image* double** — pure illusion; mirrors the core's gestures, "casts" harmlessly, draws fire.

Attacking the wrong body **wastes the attack** (image) or does little (the shard reforms — see #2).
**Which is the core is revealed only by the outs already written:** disbelief (Will vs. DC 44),
*true seeing*, **[[Cat]]'s scent** (illusions have no smell — she noses the core and the shards),
[[Briar|Briara]] sensing genuine malice, or Recall Knowledge. Until then, the party is spending actions
to find him, not to hurt him — exactly the tax a solo needs.
- **Dial:** two bodies instead of three if it's too swingy; or drop the shard's −10 turn to a single
  extra Strike.

### 2. Split Their Focus — the hostage is a *clock*, not a shield *(the real equalizer)*
A solo boss is only scary against a **reduced** party. So make the rescue *demand bodies*:
- He actively menaces the **real** [[Lyriel]] — *black tentacles* around her, or a visible round-by-round
  "execution" of the **decoy** to panic Riven — so **2–3 PCs must peel off** to find and free her while
  the rest fight. He is then effectively fighting **3–4 PCs, not 6.**
- **Worm-swarm adds** (2–3, Creature ~15–17): larval swarms he's shed. They **body-block** the lane to
  Lyriel, and they're the AoE-bait (party *wants* to fireball a swarm — but Lyriel is right there, #4).
  Guilt-free to kill (they're *him*, no Shame), which keeps him the clean no-mercy foil.
- **Dial:** the swarms are the difficulty knob — add one, or none, to taste.

### 3. Precision-Proof *(already in his kit — play it on purpose)*
**Immune to precision damage.** [[Pavel]]'s sneak attack and [[Lee]]'s Precise Strike — normally huge —
do **only their base weapon dice.** Your two front-liners can't burst him down; they're better spent on
the swarms (#2) and the rescue. Don't forget this mid-fight; it's half the reason he survives.

### 4. The AoE Tax *(reinforce his weakness against the hostage)*
His one real vulnerability — **weakness to area & splash 20** — is also the party's obvious answer to a
swarm. The **hidden Lyriel makes them pay for it:** an AoE big enough to matter risks her, or the empty
space where the decoy stands. Force the choice; don't let them free-fire.

### 5. The Lying Floor *(pre-cast terrain — the room as second combatant)*
Set the board before initiative:
- ***Illusory scene*** hides the throne room's true layout — the path the party *sees* to Lyriel/the
  decoy is not the path that exists.
- Seeded ***reverse gravity*** column(s) and ***black tentacles*** on the true approach lanes, so
  charging the decoy or the swarms crosses hazards the illusion doesn't show. First movement toward the
  bait springs it.
- **Dial:** one hazard, not two, if the room's already busy.

### 6. Reshuffle, Don't Flee *(turn the contingency into a second act)*
His **contingency *dimension door* at ≤50 HP** shouldn't be a heal-and-return speed bump. When he blinks
to M6 and returns ~1 minute later, he comes back having **reset the Shell Game and relocated Lyriel** —
new veils, new decoy position, tentacles re-seeded. The party must **re-solve which body is real and
where she is.** One escape only; the second phase is where the fight is won or the hostage is lost.

### 7. Number Tuning *(structure over inflation)*
Bump **HP 325 → ~420** so he survives the 3–4 rounds these fixes need against six PCs; leave AC 47 and
saves as printed (his defense is *deception*, not tankiness). If the party still melts him, the fix is
**more shell/hostage pressure (#1–2), not bigger numbers.**

> [!note] FoundryVTT (PF2e 8.2.0)
> The illusion bodies are best run as **separate tokens** (core + shard + image), not automation:
> keep the *project image* token's HP hidden and let it "take" hits you narrate as passing through.
> The precision immunity (#3) *is* automatable — add an **IWR: precision, immunity** rule element so the
> system auto-strips sneak-attack/precise-strike dice. Ask if you want the swarm-add statblock and the
> IWR rule element written up.

## Worm-Swarm Shard *(add — the pieces he sheds)*

The larval swarms the Wriggling Man spins off (Boss Mod #2). They are *him* — **guilt-free to kill**,
which keeps him the clean no-mercy foil — and they exist to **body-block the lane to [[Lyriel]]** and to
be the **AoE bait** (the party wants to burn a swarm, but Lyriel is right there). Field **2–3**.

**Creature 16** · Uncommon · CE · Medium · **Aberration, Swarm**

**Perception** +26; darkvision, tremorsense (imprecise) 30 ft
**Languages** understands Aklo (can't speak)
**Skills** Acrobatics +29, Stealth +28

**Str** +3, **Dex** +7, **Con** +6, **Int** −5, **Wis** +4, **Cha** +0

**AC** 38; **Fort** +29, **Ref** +28, **Will** +23
**HP** 200; **Immunities** precision, swarm mind, grabbed, prone, restrained; **Resistances** physical 10 (except area or splash); **Weaknesses** area damage 15, splash damage 15, fire 10

**Speed** 30 ft, climb 20 ft, burrow 15 ft

- **Swarm Mind** — can't be flanked; a mental effect that targets one creature affects the whole swarm as a single target; can't be individually singled out.
- **Swarming Worms** — occupies other creatures' spaces and moves through them freely; squeezes through any gap a single worm could fit.
- **Swarming Devour** ⬍⬍ (two-actions, area) — each creature in the swarm's space takes **4d8+9 piercing** (**DC 34 basic Reflex**); on a failure, the target is exposed to **Worm Rot**. *(This is the swarm's whole turn — it has no Strikes.)*
- **Worm Rot** (disease) — **DC 34 Fortitude**; **Stage 1** drained 1 (1 round); **Stage 2** drained 2 (1 round). *Feeds the boss's drain motif.*
- **Reform** ⟳ (reaction; **trigger** the shard is reduced to 0 HP while the true core still lives; **1/round**) — instead of being destroyed, the swarm scatters into the walls and floor and **re-forms at the start of the core's next turn with half its maximum HP** — **unless** the damage that dropped it was **area or splash**, in which case it is destroyed for good. *This is the AoE tax with teeth: single-target and physical chipping only delays a shard; fire and AoE actually end it — but that's exactly the damage that endangers [[Lyriel]].*

> [!note] Tuning
> Difficulty knob for the whole fight: run **1 shard** (light), **2** (default), or **3** (brutal). Bump
> to **Creature 17** by adding +1 to AC/attacks/DCs and ~+30 HP, or soften to **15** the same way. Their
> job is to *soak actions and tax AoE*, not to out-damage the boss — keep the DC under his (44).

> [!note] FoundryVTT (PF2e 8.2.0) — rule elements
> **Precision immunity** (put on the Wriggling Man **and** every shard — it's the point of Boss Mod #3;
> if you cloned the compendium *worm that walks* it's already there, but the reskinned boss needs it
> added). Rules tab → code view:
> ```json
> [
>   { "key": "Immunity", "type": "precision" }
> ]
> ```
> **Full swarm IWR** (paste onto the Worm-Swarm Shard actor so damage auto-resolves — the `exceptions`
> make physical resistance *not* apply to area/splash, so AoE lands full + weakness):
> ```json
> [
>   { "key": "Resistance", "type": "physical", "value": 10, "exceptions": ["area-damage", "splash-damage"] },
>   { "key": "Weakness", "type": "area-damage", "value": 15 },
>   { "key": "Weakness", "type": "splash-damage", "value": 15 },
>   { "key": "Weakness", "type": "fire", "value": 10 },
>   { "key": "Immunity", "type": "precision" },
>   { "key": "Immunity", "type": "swarm-mind" }
> ]
> ```
> **Reform** isn't cleanly automatable (it's conditional on the *damage category* that dropped the token).
> Run it by hand: when a shard hits 0, check the killing blow — area/splash → dead; anything else →
> re-add at half HP on the core's turn. Leave the token on the scene at 0 HP as your reminder.

## Tactics (Throne Room K17) *(vanilla — superseded by the redesign above; kept for reference)*

He sets up an elaborate deception before the PCs arrive:

1. Casts *illusory scene* to hide the north end of the throne room and make it look like statues are flanking the throne.
2. Hides behind the throne (north of illusion).
3. When PCs enter: rolls Stealth initiative, then uses **time stop** (3 free rounds):
   - Round 1: *veil* to appear as an 8-ft golden-skinned angel
   - Round 2: *ventriloquism*
   - Round 3: *project image* of "himself" (the angel) 30 ft south in the room's center

4. The "angel" appears and tries to convince PCs to leave: *"Heaven has come to contain this place, you are no longer needed."*
5. If the ruse works and PCs leave, he follows invisibly to confirm they're gone.
6. If they see through it, he sustains *project image* and casts offensive spells through the illusion.

**Contingency**: At ≤50 HP, *dimension door* to area M6 (garden) to recover, then returns to finish the fight to the death.

If he incapacitates a PC, he cannot resist using **Squirming Embrace** on them.
