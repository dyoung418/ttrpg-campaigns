# Lady Jamandi Aldori

> **Role**: Noble patron (Brevoy/Restov); the party's original charter-grantor; secret ally
> **Location**: Brevoy (Restov)
> **Status**: Alive

## Description

A political operator from Restov, Brevoy — sharp, cautious, and now impressed despite herself by what the "motley six" have built.

## Personality

Strategic, measured, self-aware. She acknowledges her own blind spots: she missed how successful Freehaven would become because she was too focused on Brevoy's political intrigue. Her money had always been on [[Maegar Varn]] as the most likely to succeed — and she had considered a strategic marriage alliance with him. Now he is dead and Varnhold in tatters.

She is worried. Nyrissa, the rising danger from the south (Irovetti's ambition), the Iron Wraiths going dark — she sees threats converging. And she is starting to think Freehaven's salvation may be intertwined with Restov's.

## Background

Lady Jamandi Aldori of Brevoy invited the PCs to settle the lawless Stolen Lands and granted them a charter to do so. She also issued a charter to [[Hannis Drevlev]] and others. The PCs first had to defeat the [[Stag Lord]] before they could establish their barony, which eventually became [[Freehaven]].

She attended the **Great Feast of Freehaven** in secret — hooded and glamoured, on [[Pavel]]'s watch — to assess Freehaven firsthand. Her internal verdict: she had badly underestimated the six. She was astonished by their diplomatic successes (the Nomen Centaurs! The economy rivaling Restov!), and embarrassed that she'd dismissed them as "good in a fight" who might struggle to rule. She now sees drawing closer to Freehaven as potentially more important than the political battles in Brevoy.

She wants to forge a closer alliance with Freehaven without triggering backlash from Brevoy's reactionary factions, who are suspicious of Restov aligning with southern kingdoms.

## Intelligence Shared with Riven

**On Irovetti (pre-defeat):** Her agents uncovered that Castruccio Irovetti was not his birth name. He was born the illegitimate son of a father he never met; his mother died when he was in his early twenties. His birth name was believed to be **Mandalarucio**. His past intersects with the **Technic League**, which may explain the name change. He came into possession of powerful devices — possibly Technic League in origin — which were instrumental in his rise to power. He worries her: a man who came from nothing with a shady past and technological power that can topple kingdoms.

**On Brevoy:** There is strong suspicion among reactionary elements in the north about Restov aligning with southern kingdoms. Handling the Freehaven alliance requires discretion.

**On Pitax (her stance):** She keeps relations with Pitax "cordial but cautious, arms length" and does not share the concern some in Brevoy have about that front.

## Relationships

- [[Riven]] — issued him and his companions their charter; now meeting as peers; shared intelligence and discussed alliance
- [[Hannis Drevlev]] — also issued a charter (he later turned against Freehaven)
- [[Maegar Varn]] — her favored charterholder; she had considered a strategic marriage alliance with him; he is now dead

## GM Notes

- Lady Jamandi is now actively moving toward an alliance with Freehaven — she sees it as strategically essential, not just courtesy.
- The secrecy of her visit matters: if it became known she attended the feast, it could inflame Brevoy's reactionaries.
- She is scared of Nyrissa — and doesn't know where to start to address the threat beyond drawing closer to the six.

## Session History

- First appeared: Early campaign (charter-granting feast — she was nearly killed there; the party helped save her)
- Great Feast of Freehaven: Attended in disguise (hooded + glamoured); shared Irovetti intelligence; discussed alliance with Riven
