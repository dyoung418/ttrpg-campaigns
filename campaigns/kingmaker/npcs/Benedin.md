# Benedin

> **Role**: Dwarven companion; lorekeeper-in-training; community figure
> **Location**: [[Bronzeshield Hold]]
> **Faction**: [[Freehaven]]
> **Status**: Alive
![[Benedin - cutout.webp]]
## Description

Wears spectacles. Pushes them back to the bridge of his nose when frustrated or gathering his thoughts.

## Personality

Measured in judgment — he will condemn those who abandon their duty, but not those who perish upholding it. Draws that line clearly and stands behind it. Gets defensive when broad judgments are cast against fallen leaders; his clan's own fall to an unexpected attack is still raw.

Pauses to "chew on thoughts" before speaking; cools quickly once he's made his point. Empathizes even when he's disagreeing.

*"Aye, duty is important. I'll judge those that abandon it, but not those who perish in upholding it."*

## Background

Benedin's clan had a stronghold in the mountains of the eastern Stolen Lands long before Aldori was a household name. His parents ruled the clan, and life was stable and predictable. He was being trained as the clan's **lorekeeper** — the one who would guide and preserve the clan's knowledge when his time came.

Then the trolls came. They were unlike anything the clan had prepared for. The trolls wielded magic: veils and glamours, and the ability to reshape stone itself to create openings in the hold's defenses. The clan had prepared to withstand a conventional siege. They were not prepared for this. The hold fell and the dwarves were driven into exile.

Years later, the PCs cleared the hold — defeating [[Hargulka]] — and invited the exiled dwarven community to return. Benedin was central to preparing [[Bronzeshield Hold]] for the clan's homecoming. He remained with the party as a traveling companion for much of the campaign and is now a figure within the community.

## Relationships

- [[Hyram]] — his animal companion; a very large goat; Benedin rides Hyrum into battle
- [[Riven]] — deep respect; Riven sees Benedin as someone who has kept going after loss; has asked him about his people
- [[Pavel]], [[Astor]], [[Lucas]], [[Lee]], [[Milo]] — long-time travel companions
- [[Bronzeshield Hold]] — his ancestral home; his people
- [[Hargulka]] — the troll king whose clan drove Benedin's people into exile; now defeated

## Notable Moments

- **The staircase at Vordakai's lair**: Listened to the party's debate about purpose and leadership approvingly. He noted privately that [[Lucas]] was bringing perspective he wasn't sure the leaders of Freehaven had. [[Lucas]] concluded by explicitly deferring to Benedin: *"Talk with Benedin. He's a man who knows how to lead."* Benedin is seen — by Lucas at least — as the most natural leader in the group despite not holding a PC role.

- **"There's a way to trap someone forever." (campfire at the Varnhold camp — post-Vordakai)**: When [[Riven]] asked what everyone was hoping for, Benedin reported: [[Linzi]]'s article on Bronzeshield had worked — some young cousins (<100 years old) had moved in ("mostly useless, the lot, but they'll wisen up"). But a new threat had arrived: an arsonist — a "red-haired devil" — who has been emboldened by the party's absence. Benedin is unsure how to deal with them; rituals could snare innocents, and he's not good at lying in wait. He plans to stay home while the party plans the next step, hoping his presence will deter the arsonist or get lucky enough to catch them. He then described what he'd found in the *Eclipsed Incantations*: a binding circle that may explain the night demons; and — in a conspiratorial whisper — a ritual that could trap someone *permanently*. *"Most everything has a weakness, but some things just can't die. This is a way to put them away, for good."* His afterlife view emerged: he believes in joining one's gods in their celestial domain and would find eternal imprisonment the worst possible fate.

- **The Nomen Heights walk (with Riven and Pavel)**: When [[Riven]] condemned the Varnhold leadership for failing their people, Benedin pushed back firmly: *"That's a big judgement to cast. We hardly know the story of their fall."* He drew a direct parallel: *"If it were your town that had vanished, you with it, would you want to be judged the same?"* His defensiveness was visible — almost certainly because his own clan's fall to an unexpected magical attack resonates. When Riven later asked him about his people, he shared the story: the stronghold, the lorekeeper training, the troll attack with strange magic (veils, glamours, stone-reshaping), the exile.

## The Eclipsed Incantations

A powerful magical tome [[Benedin]] is currently studying. Contains at least two significant rituals:
- **Binding circle** — may explain the "night demons" plaguing [[Bronzeshield Hold]]; could also be repurposed to summon more benevolent spirits
- **Permanent imprisonment ritual** — a way to trap someone or something forever, with no death. Benedin describes it as *"a way to put them away, for good."* He notes it could be relevant since "some things just can't die." [[Pavel]] called it "a fate worse than death."

## GM Notes

> [!secret] GM Only
> - **Fully NPC as of Session 100.** Previously played by Slava (who also played [[Eri Malai]]); Slava has returned to [[Astor]] for the remainder of the campaign. Benedin is now a background NPC at [[Bronzeshield Hold]] — present by letter, cameo, or offscreen reference.
> - Benedin's defensiveness about judging fallen leaders is a well of roleplay potential: when other communities fail or are destroyed, he will instinctively defend them.
> - His lorekeeper training makes him the keeper of clan history — he knows things about the dwarven presence in this region that others don't.
> - The magic the trolls used to take the hold was unusual — veils, glamours, reshaping stone. Was that just Hargulka's particular clan, or something more? Worth exploring if relevant.
>
> - **"You're part of us now." (campfire on the road back from Drevlev)**: Wounded deep by [[Ovinrbaane]] in the Drevlev fight — Lucas and Pavel treated him masterfully, but he was still winking as he sat down. He leaned against [[Hyram]], who had already curled up to sleep. After dinner (Milo's dinner rolls, inexplicably perfect), he cleared his throat and told the group he'd been meaning to thank them — all of them. He struggled to find the words, then landed on the difference between his upbringing and what he'd found in this group: he grew up knowing favors must be repaid, the more help you receive the more you owe, and he'd gotten the sense the party doesn't keep score. He named specific debts: they'd beaten him to Hargulka but then recognized his ancestral claim to the Hold after bleeding for it; they'd rebuilt the keep and spread word so his surviving cousins could find their way home.
>
>   Then he announced: he was leaving. He wanted to return to [[Bronzeshield Hold]] — properly. He'd been brandishing the crown regalia in battle but had never worn it. He hadn't earned that right yet. He needed to be among his people to have a chance at it.
>
>   [[Pavel]] tried to argue that Hyrum wouldn't be happy there. The Hold smells like smoke, leather, metal polish, forges, sweaty dwarf. *"Industry and people."* He nodded emphatically as if he'd made a compelling point. When Benedin just looked back at him, Pavel dropped his gaze: *"Why? You're part of us now."*
>
>   Benedin met his eye and held it even when Pavel looked away. Then: *"You're right, of course. I am one of you now, and I think I always will be. And I'm glad you've been to Bronzeshield Hold. I'll see what I can do about the smell."* He then gave Pavel a lesson — slowly enunciated, as if to help it stick: for a demon, you want cold iron; for a devil, you want silver. *"I'll write it down. It'll be clearer."*
>
>   After Riven's speech, Benedin offered his own farewell to the group: he named what Riven had grown into — a leader who turns few away, who believes all people can live together, who manages culture clashes with respect. *"Whenever I feel stubborn, I'll remember your open-mindedness."* He added plans: kitchen improvements, new herbs for the garden, wards to block any scrying sensors. Milo can expect engineering questions by letter.
