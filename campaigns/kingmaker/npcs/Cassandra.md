# Cassandra

> **Role**: Silverstep Guard — captain
> **Location**: [[Silverstep]] (buried on the lake)
> **Faction**: [[Freehaven]]
> **Status**: **Deceased** — died at the [[Willowshade]] valley ford, Pitax War

## Description

Braid over one shoulder, halberd in hand. She moved among her wounded personally — tearing cloth with her own hands to bind wounds, steadying voices, staying visible. She didn't hold apart from her Guard; she was one of them.

## Personality

She read the numbers clearly and didn't flinch from what they told her. At the meadow skirmish, three dead and five wounded against sixty mercenaries — she called the tactical truth without self-deception: *"They did not have men enough to stop Pitax's weight."* And then she kept moving. She had a soldier's discipline about hope: she'd stand until the last, and she knew when the last was coming.

She also knew contempt when she saw it — knew it from inside. When she described [[Lord Darian]] to [[Riven]], she said: *"I know the look. I wore it once. Steel in my hands, sure the world couldn't touch me. It blinds you."* She used that knowledge to build the trap that killed him.

## Backstory

Once followed the [[The Stag Lord|Stag Lord]], certain her life would end nameless in a ditch. [[Freehaven]]'s founding gave her something she hadn't counted on: *a chance she had never earned.* She bore that trust like armor. She built the Silverstep Guard from it.

Darian, who held her in contempt the night before Willowshade, called her "a thief, a bandit in the service of the Stag Lord." He was right about her past. He was wrong about what it made her.

## Background

**Great Feast of Freehaven:** Intercepted [[Riven]] to share news that the Silverstep Guard had developed new techniques with their polearms. She urged him to visit. He promised he would.

**The Battle of the Silverstep Meadow (Day 1):** With twenty Guard, Cassandra met the sixty-man vanguard of the [[Golden Talons]] at the hedgerow ditch on the meadow road. She directed a volley, braced the halberds, and fought [[Lord Darian]]'s column to a standstill before giving the ordered retreat — two at a time, covering each other. Three dead, five wounded. She looked at the dead in the meadow, read the numbers, and made her preparations.

**Council in the Dark:** [[Riven]] arrived in the night with thirty Rangers — he had sent [[Ekundayo]] north to reinforce [[Serenko]] with the other Rangers. Cassandra met him across a charcoal-scratched map with two sergeants, [[Halvik]] (broad, blunt) and [[Senn]] (lean, cautious). She described Darian to Riven: *"He rides as if the ground already belongs to him. He doesn't see us — only through us. To him, we're rabble in stolen armor. He'll drive hard the moment he thinks the line bends."* She named the plan: *"Then we gamble the city on [[Willowshade]]."*

**The Battle of Willowshade:** She walked before the Guard in the meadow mist: *"Close ranks. Step when I step."* She led the feigned retreat across the ford in perfect order, trading ground for depth, drawing the Talons deeper into the hollow. She held the cadence — *"Step. Hold. Step."* — until [[Riven]]'s arrows came from the ridges and the storm broke on the Talons. She called the wheel — *"Wheel!"* — and the Guard pivoted across the valley's throat.

A spear slid under her arm and burst through her ribs.

She drove her halberd home anyway — hooking a shield rim, dragging it down so [[Halvik]] beside her could thrust. Her knees buckled. The sergeant dragged her back.

**Death:** She lay against the sergeant's arm, breath rattling, eyes on the ford. [[Riven]] crouched beside her and tried twice to heal her. The green light flickered, guttered, and died. She met his eyes.

*"Don't,"* she rasped.

He let the magic fade.

*"You set the trap and won the day,"* he said.

*"Then… don't waste it."*

Her hand slackened from the haft. Her gaze dulled.

**Ash on the Lake:** The Guard carried her home on a bier of crossed spears. At the steps of Desna's temple, they laid her halberd across her chest and stood bareheaded beside their weapons. Riven spoke: *"She spent her life on this field. We owe it to her and the others who fell today to live true and fight on."* That night, her body was set on a boat on Lake Silverstep. The fire took slow hold, smoke trailing low across the water.
