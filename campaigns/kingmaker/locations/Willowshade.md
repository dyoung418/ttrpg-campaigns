# Willowshade

> **Region**: Stolen Lands — a valley hollow between [[Silverstep]] and the surrounding hills
> **Type**: Natural terrain — valley, ford, ridges
> **Status**: Battlefield; the decisive site of the Pitax War's assault on Silverstep

## Description

A hollow where the meadow road crosses a ford, the valley narrows between two pine-covered ridges before opening toward [[Silverstep]]. The ridges run close and steep enough to command the ground below. The current runs cold and strong enough that men crossing at the knee in armor struggle. In winter conditions the valley becomes a trap: wind funnels through the hollow, sleet slicks the earth to mud, and hail hammers anything exposed in the open.

Named by [[Cassandra]] the night before the battle: *"Then we gamble the city on Willowshade."*

## The Battle of Willowshade (Pitax War)

[[Riven]] devised the plan in the pine grove outside [[Silverstep]], with [[Cassandra]], [[Halvik]], and [[Senn]] huddled over a charcoal map. Forty halberds feigned strength in the meadow, drew the [[Golden Talons]] forward under [[Lord Darian]], then gave ground slowly across the ford in good order — making Darian believe they were breaking. He drove the Talons hard into the valley.

Once the Talons were in the hollow, [[Riven]]'s Rangers loosed from both ridges. Officers fell. Drummers fell. Then the storm — hail, sleet, blizzard wind — Riven's druid magic turning the hollow into a killing ground. The Talons' discipline shattered. [[Cassandra]] wheeled the Guard across the valley's throat to catch the fugitives. [[Lord Darian]] was killed by a flaming arrow through the breastplate. Two hundred men dissolved into rout.

**The price:** [[Cassandra]] was speared through the ribs while holding the wheel. She died in the valley. [[Riven]] tried twice to heal her with magic and could not.

## Significance

- The battle that broke the Golden Talons and ended the Pitax War's threat to [[Silverstep]]
- Where [[Cassandra]] died
- Where [[Lord Darian]] died
- The Silverstep Guard does not speak much of it, but they know what it cost
