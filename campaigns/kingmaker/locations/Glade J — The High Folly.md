# Glade J — The High Folly

> **Type**: Fey glade (Thousandbreaths)
> **Status**: Hostile
> **Anchor**: [[Ilthuliak]]
> **Parent**: [[Thousandbreaths]]

## Summary

Mossy stone statues of a petrified First World army surrounding a 300-foot white stone tower. [[Ilthuliak]] perches atop the tower. The acid lake below; a breath weapon (22d6 acid, 100-ft line) from a creature that circles overhead.

## Three Aspects

- **The petrified army** — a First World host frozen mid-charge in mossy stone; climbable terrain that lets PCs gain height to *reach* a flying foe. **Ilthuliak can wake them as her risen trophies** — see the Frozen Vanguard block in [[Session 102 - The Undefeated]].
- **[[Ilthuliak]] atop the white tower** — she sees intruders coming and opens with *time stop*; she circles and kites, swooping only when her breath recharges.
- **The acid lake** — Ilthuliak is immune; the party is not. Her signature play is putting them *in* it (*reverse gravity*, Grab-and-drop). It also doubles as a no-HP-kill lever the party can turn against the woken host.

## The Petrified Host

The army came, generations ago, to end Nyrissa's sorrow and got only as far as the dragon. Ilthuliak broke them and keeps them as stone — her "never lost" record standing guard. She raises them with her **Animate Troops** action (3 at a time, recharge 1d4 rounds), so the host is a *renewable tap she controls* — the army is effectively endless while she lives, which makes killing her the win condition. **When Ilthuliak dies, her will lets go and every animated soldier falls still again.**

### The Charge Everlasting — Frozen Vanguard (Creature 15)

*A First World honor-charge, lances couched, frozen at the instant they broke against the tower. Ilthuliak's will pulls them upright — they resume the assault they died making, indifferent to who stands in the way.*

- **The Charge Everlasting** — if it Strides ≥10 ft before a Strike, that Strike deals **+2d12** (~46 total) and the target makes a **DC 33 Fortitude** save or is knocked prone and pushed 5 ft.
- **Shatterstone** ⚡ — when it drops to 0 HP **or is critically hit**, it bursts: **6d6**, 10-ft emanation, **DC 33 basic Reflex**.

**Why they're hard *and* fair:**
- **Mindless → immune to mental effects** (e.g. [[Lucas]]'s fascination). They must be broken the hard way. [[Ilthuliak]] herself is *not* immune.
- **Hardness 8** rewards big hits and crits; chip damage feels like nothing.
- **No-HP-kill levers:** Trip/Shove/grapple aren't mental — shove a soldier into the **acid lake** or off a tower ledge to delete it. Ilthuliak's own *reverse gravity* flings them too.
