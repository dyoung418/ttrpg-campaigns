# Thousandbreaths

[[Nyrissa]]'s personal demiplane — a prison and power base. Entered via the Castle of Knives portal in the Thousand Voices forest (requires **3+ different bloom trophies**). PCs always arrive at the Byway (Area A).

## Demiplane Properties

- **Eternal Twilight** — Always dim light. The sun never moves.
- **The Woods** — Off-path travel is greater difficult terrain AND hazardous (1d6 piercing per Move action from thorns).
- **Sounds** — Whispering voices, cries, possibly familiar voices. −2 circumstance penalty on Perception checks to hear things.
- **Sleeping here** — Triggers living nightmares as long as the Nightmare Rook (Glade D) still lives.
- **Flying above treetops** — Impossible. The canopy extends to 200 feet no matter how high you fly. Only Nyrissa and the Nightmare Rook can fly above the trees.
- **River travel** — Always moves downstream (toward Glade B). Rivers flow from Glade K toward Glade B.
- **Path travel** — 1d4 hours to reach the next glade. Each path transit: roll DC 11 flat check; on success, roll Pathway Perils (see below).

## Pathway Perils (d4)

1. Statues of human-animal hybrids, fleshy trees with grafted people who speak nonsense. DC 38 Will save or confused for 1d6 rounds.
2. Narrow path (10 feet wide). 2d4 hours instead of 1d4 to reach next glade.
3. Strange writhing flowers with tentacle vines. Halfway down, party is exposed to nightmare vapor.
4. Animal carcasses everywhere. Exposed to virulent bubonic plague (18th-level disease, DC 38 Fort save).

## Uprooting Thousandbreaths

Each time the PCs defeat all guardians of a glade, roll a flat DC 20 check — on a success, Thousandbreaths uproots (manifests in the Thousand Voices forest). On a failure, reduce the DC by the glade's value for next time. **If all anchors are defeated, it uproots automatically.**

| Glade | Guardian | DC Reduction |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| B | 4× Storm-Struck Arboreals | −1 |
| C | Kargstaad *(skipped by GM decision)* | −2 |
| D | Nightmare Rook | −2 |
| E | Immense Mandragora | −1 |
| G | The Wriggling Man (area K17) | −4 |
| H | The Knurly Witch | −4 |
| I | Destroy the Baleful Lantern | −1 |
| J | Ilthuliak | −4 |

When uprooted: all active blooms in the Stolen Lands end instantly. Nyrissa can no longer use the Apology to absorb the kingdom. She shifts to vengeance mode (daily primal call rituals). +120 XP story award.

## The Nine Glades

### A. The Byway
Entry point for all PCs. Iron arch formed by two women-statues, each raising a sword resembling [[Briar]]. First time Briar passes through: +1 Sharpness Point, both statues and the sword glow green. Nyrissa notices but does not react.

### B. Watchers on the Whirling Shore (Moderate 18)
Dark lake with 100-ft diameter silent whirlpool (DC 38 Reflex to avoid being swept). Black swans glide harmlessly. **Anchors**: 4× Storm-Struck Arboreals (CR 16) — immune to electricity, healed by it; weakness to axes. One guards each exit.
*Bloom trophy*: Black swan carcasses (linked to Whirling Lake bloom).

### C. The Frozen Boneyard (Moderate 18)
Extreme cold environment. Oversized cemetery with 10-ft headstones, a leaning tower, and a marble vault. **Anchor**: [[Kargstaad]] (CR 19), four-armed frost giant in the vault; sends his 6 giants to the Frozen Bloom first, then waits here.
*Bloom trophy*: Icy gravestone shards (linked to Frozen Bloom).

> [!info] GM decision (2026-07-02): this glade is being skipped
> Kargstaad will not be run; the party bypasses the Frozen Boneyard. Note for the uprooting mechanic: Glade C's −2 DC reduction (and its contribution to "all anchors defeated → automatic uproot") is off the table unless plans change.

### D. The Nightmare Spire (Moderate 18)
Mile-wide clearing, jagged hills, thorny plains. A stone spire (appears mile-tall, actually 200 ft). **Anchor**: [[The Nightmare Rook]] (CR 20) — a gargantuan bird. Primary tactic: Snatch PCs and drop them into other glades (Mire or Fruiting Orchard). Carries passengers only at Nyrissa's request. At <90 HP, lies about ferrying PCs to safety (drops them in Mire/Orchard).
*Bloom trophy*: Black raven feathers (linked to Nights of Dread bloom).

### E. The Fruiting Orchard (Moderate 18)
30-ft brick wall surrounding a garden of mushrooms, tropical plants, and bulging "fruits" (growing mandragoras). **Anchor**: Immense Mandragora (CR 20) — hundreds of thousands of small mandragoras coalesce into one 35-ft giant.
*Bloom trophy*: Dead mandragoras (linked to Shrieking Children bloom).

### F. The Shunned Falls (Moderate 18)
Waterfall between carved stone hands into a crystal pool. **The one safe rest area in Thousandbreaths** — Nyrissa hates it, won't intrude. Fireflies here are sacred to the Lantern King (DC 35 First World Lore/Nature/Religion). Large leaves float as rafts.
*No associated bloom*. Creatures: 2× Winged Owlbears (CR 18) — territorial pair, not Nyrissa's agents.

### G. The Mire (Moderate 18)
Foul-smelling marsh, about a mile across. Average 10 ft deep with 30-ft troughs; ridges just below surface. Moving along ridges: DC 30 Acrobatics or fall into 30-ft trough. The [[The Wriggling Man|Wriggling Man]] normally lives here but is in the House. **Anchor**: Wriggling Man (his glade tether, fought in area K17).
*Bloom trophy*: Twitching worms (linked to Rise of the Mire Worms bloom).

### H. The Knurly House (Moderate 18)
Two dozen houses tangled together. Thorny yard: 20 ft thick, 15 ft tall, 10-ft clear path. Off-path: difficult + hazardous terrain (4d6 piercing per Move action). **Anchor**: [[The Knurly Witch]] (CR 20) annis hag, cleric of Gyronna. Invites PCs to "tea" (crow's blood + powdered maggots; DC 38 Fort or sickened 2–4). Exchanges lies for information. Uses *word of recall* to flee to K16 in the House at <100 HP.
*Bloom trophy*: Two-foot knotted brambles (linked to Knurly Briars bloom).

### I. The Baleful Lantern (Moderate 18)
Beehive-shaped structure lit by decapitated human heads used as lanterns (necromantic). The Misbegotten Troll serves as normal tether, but during Chapter 10 the tether transfers to the **building itself**. Destroying the Baleful Lantern (AC 30, Hardness 20, HP 400, BT 200) breaks the anchor. If <BT: DC 43 Athletics to Force Open load-bearing bars and collapse it (20d6 bludgeoning, DC 35 Reflex, 30-ft area). Guarded by 8× Black Smilodons (CR 14).
*Bloom trophy*: Broken cages (linked to Misbegotten Troll bloom).

### J. The High Folly (Severe 18)
Mossy stone statues (petrified First World army, restored by Day 26 bloom). A 300-ft white stone tower. **Anchor**: [[Ilthuliak]] (CR 21), black dragon, atop the tower. Starts combat with *time stop* → *disappearance* + *spell turning* + *true seeing* → breath weapon.
*Bloom trophy*: Handfuls of sand (linked to War of the High Folly bloom).

### K. Castle of Knives (Portal Site)
Not a glade but the entry/exit point. The fixed portal to Thousandbreaths in the Thousand Voices forest. Requires 3+ bloom trophies to activate. The iron gate flanked by women-statues functions as the portal as long as Thousandbreaths exists.

## Researching Thousandbreaths

Library Research checks (DC 33–40 depending on skill) award Research Points unlocking intel about the realm (2 RP through 18 RP). Need **18 RP** to use *plane shift* to travel directly. Key unlocks:
- 4 RP: Nyrissa rules Thousandbreaths
- 14 RP: Castle of Knives is the portal; 9 lesser glades + prime glade (House at the Edge of Time)
- 18 RP: Defeating anchors uproots the realm
